**Country Name: Madagascar**

**🇲🇬 Madagascar – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Code of Madagascar (Code du Travail)_ – contracts, rights, termination.
    *   _Social Security Code_.
    *   _Occupational Safety and Health Regulations_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour, Employment, Public Service, and Social Laws.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.
*   Recent Developments (2025–26): Labour Code modernization to attract foreign investment and strengthen compliance frameworks.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (Contrat à Durée Indéterminée – CDI).
    *   Fixed‑term (Contrat à Durée Déterminée – CDD, max 2 years, renewable).
    *   Temporary/seasonal work.
*   Probationary Periods: Typically 3–6 months depending on role.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Contracts must be in French or Malagasy.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (8 hours/day).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.3×_ hourly wage → weekdays.
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → weekends/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 24 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   2.5 working days/month (≈30 days/year).
*   Public Holidays: 11 annually (e.g., Independence Day, Christmas, Easter).
*   Sick Leave: Paid via social security; employer covers initial period.
*   Maternity Leave: 14 weeks (paid).
*   Paternity Leave: 10 days (paid).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage (SMIG): Adjusted periodically (~MGA 250,000/month in 2025).
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (employer ~13%, employee ~5%).
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common under CBAs.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   15 days (< 1 year of Service)
    *   1 month (1–5 years).
    *   2 months (≥5 years).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   10 days’ wages per year of service (redundancy or unfair dismissal).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate: Oversees compliance.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.
