**Country Name: Chile**

**🇨🇱 Chile – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Code (Código del Trabajo)_ – main employment law.
    *   _Social Security Law_.
    *   _Occupational Safety and Health Regulations_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour and Social Welfare.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.
*   Recent Developments (2025–26):
*   Gradual reduction of maximum weekly working hours.
*   Strengthened parental leave entitlements.
*   Expanded digital payroll and compliance systems.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment: Permanent, fixed‑term, temporary, apprenticeship.
*   Probationary Periods: Not formally regulated; trial periods must be contractual.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Spanish mandatory.
*   Written Contracts: Required for all employees.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (reduced from 45 hours by 2025 reform).
*   Overtime Rules: Paid at _1.5×_ hourly wage.
*   Rest Periods: Weekly rest day (Sunday).

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave: 15 working days/year (after 1 year of service).
*   Public Holidays: ~15 annually (including Independence Day, Christmas, New Year, National Holidays).
*   Sick Leave: Paid via social security (medical certificate required).
*   Maternity Leave: 18 weeks (6 weeks pre‑natal, 12 weeks post‑natal, paid).
*   Paternity Leave: 5 days (paid), plus shared parental leave options.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted annually (~CLP 500,000/month in 2025).
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Pension fund (AFP): employee ~10%.
    *   Health insurance: employee ~7%.
    *   Unemployment insurance: employer ~2.4%, employee ~0.6%.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in CBAs.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods: 30 days (unless payment in lieu).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   1 month’s wages per year of service (capped at 11 months).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation (usually 30 days).
*   Employees must contribute to pension and health systems.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate: Oversees compliance.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.

**9\. References**

*   Ministry of Labour and Social Welfare (Chile) – official portal.

*   **Labour Code (Código del Trabajo)**: Core employment law.
*   **Social Security Law**: Governs pension, health, unemployment insurance.
*   **Occupational Safety and Health Regulations**: Workplace safety standards.
*   **Language Requirement**: Contracts must be in Spanish.

*   Reduction of maximum weekly working hours from 45 to 40.
*   Strengthened parental leave entitlements.
