**Country Name: Columbia**

**🇨🇴 Colombia – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Colombian Labour Code (Código Sustantivo del Trabajo)_ – contracts, rights, termination.
    *   _Law 100 of 1993_ – social security system.
    *   _Law 50 of 1990_ – employment reforms.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour (Ministerio del Trabajo).
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (indefinite term).
    *   Fixed‑term (max 3 years, renewable).
    *   Temporary/seasonal work.
    *   Apprenticeship contracts.
*   Probationary Periods: Max 2 months (indefinite contracts); max 1/5 of contract length (fixed‑term).
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Spanish mandatory.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 48 hours/week (recent reforms allow flexible distribution).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.25×_ hourly wage → overtime weekdays.
    *   _1.75×_ hourly wage → night overtime.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → Sundays/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 24 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   15 working days/year minimum.
*   Public Holidays: 18 annually (e.g., Independence Day, Christmas, Easter, religious holidays).
*   Sick Leave: Paid via social security (EPS) from day 3 onward; employer covers first 2 days.
*   Maternity Leave: 18 weeks (paid at 100%).
*   Paternity Leave: 2 weeks (paid).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted annually (~COP 1,300,000/month in 2025).
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (employer ~29.5%, employee ~8%).
    *   Covers pension, health, occupational risks, family welfare.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay (Prima de Servicios): Mandatory — paid in June and December.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   Not required for indefinite contracts; compensation applies instead.
*   Severance Pay:
    *   30 days’ salary for first year of service.
    *   20 days’ salary per additional year (for employees earning
    *   20 days’ salary for first year + 15 days per additional year (for employees earning ≥10 minimum wages).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation (usually 15–30 days).
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Ministry of Labour: Oversees compliance.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.
