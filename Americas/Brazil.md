**Country Name: Brazil**

**🇧🇷 Brazil – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Consolidation of Labour Laws (CLT – Decreto‑Lei No. 5,452/1943)_ – contracts, rights, termination.
    *   _Federal Constitution (1988)_ – fundamental labour rights.
    *   _Labour Reform Law No. 13,467/2017_ – modernized contracts, union rules, working hours.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour and Employment; Labour Courts.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs are highly influential and often override statutory minimums.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (default).
    *   Fixed‑term (max 2 years).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/seasonal work.
    *   Intermittent contracts (introduced in 2017 reform).
*   Probationary Periods: Max 90 days.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Portuguese mandatory.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 44 hours/week (8 hours/day, 5.5 days).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime weekdays.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → Sundays/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 24 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   30 calendar days/year minimum.
*   Public Holidays: 12 nationally (plus state/municipal holidays).
*   Sick Leave: Employer pays first 15 days; then social security (INSS) covers.
*   Maternity Leave: 120 days (paid, extendable to 180 days under “Empresa Cidadã” program).
*   Paternity Leave: 5 days (extendable to 20 days under “Empresa Cidadã”).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted annually (~R$ 1,412/month in 2025).
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (INSS): employer ~20%, employee 7.5–14%.
    *   Severance Indemnity Fund (FGTS): employer 8%.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay (Décimo Terceiro Salário): Mandatory — paid in two installments (Nov & Dec).

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   30 days minimum, plus 3 days per year of service (max 90 days).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   FGTS balance + 40% penalty on FGTS deposits (if employer terminates without cause).
    *   Reduced penalty (20%) for mutual termination agreements.
*   Exemptions: No FGTS penalty if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Employees must contribute to INSS.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate: Oversees compliance.
*   Labour Courts (Justiça do Trabalho): Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.

**🇧🇷 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **CLT (Consolidation of Labour Laws)**: Governs contracts, rights, termination.
*   **Federal Constitution (1988)**: Establishes fundamental labour rights.
*   **Labour Reform Law No. 13,467/2017**: Introduced intermittent contracts, flexible working, union reforms.
*   **Language Requirement**: Contracts must be in Portuguese.
*   **Recent Developments (2025–26)**:
*   Annual minimum wage adjustments continue due to inflation.
*   FGTS withdrawal rules updated for certain categories (e.g., housing, unemployment).
*   Digital labour inspection and compliance systems expanded.
