**Country Name: Mexico**

**🇲🇽 Mexico – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Federal Labour Law (Ley Federal del Trabajo – LFT)_ – contracts, rights, termination.
    *   _Mexican Constitution (Article 123)_ – fundamental labour rights.
    *   _Social Security Law (Ley del Seguro Social)_ – benefits and contributions.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour and Social Welfare (Secretaría del Trabajo y Previsión Social – STPS).
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.
*   Recent Developments (2023–25):
*   Reduction of maximum weekly working hours from 48 to 40 (phased implementation).
*   Expansion of mandatory vacation entitlements (2023 reform).
*   Strengthening of profit‑sharing rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (indefinite term).
    *   Fixed‑term (allowed if justified).
    *   Seasonal/temporary.
    *   Apprenticeship contracts.
*   Probationary Periods: Max 30 days (extendable to 180 days for management/technical roles).
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Spanish mandatory.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (recent reform; previously 48).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → first 9 overtime hours/week.
    *   _3×_ hourly wage → beyond 9 hours/week.
*   Rest Periods: 1 day weekly rest (usually Sunday).

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave (Vacation):
    *   12 working days (after 1 year of service).
    *   Increases by 2 days per year until 20 days (after 5 years).
    *   After 6 years, increases by 2 days every 5 years.
*   Public Holidays: 8–10 annually (e.g., Constitution Day, Independence Day, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: Paid via social security (IMSS) from day 4 onward; employer covers first 3 days.
*   Maternity Leave: 12 weeks (6 before, 6 after birth, paid at 100%).
*   Paternity Leave: 5 working days (paid).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted annually (~MXN 249/day in 2025; higher in Free Trade Zone of Northern Border).
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (IMSS): employer ~15–25%, employee ~2–3%.
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Profit Sharing (PTU): Mandatory — 10% of company’s taxable income distributed among employees.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay (Aguinaldo): Mandatory — minimum 15 days’ salary, paid by December 20.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods: Not required; compensation applies instead.
*   Severance Pay (Unjustified Dismissal):
    *   3 months’ salary.
    *   20 days’ salary per year of service.
    *   Seniority premium: 12 days’ salary per year of service (capped at twice minimum wage).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation (usually 15 days).
*   Employees must contribute to IMSS.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   STPS (Ministry of Labour): Oversees compliance.
*   Labour Courts (Tribunales Laborales): Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.
