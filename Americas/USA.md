**Country Name: USA**

**🇺🇸 United States – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)_ – wages, hours, overtime.
    *   _Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)_ – unpaid leave.
    *   _Occupational Safety and Health Act (OSHA)_ – workplace safety.
    *   _Civil Rights Act (Title VII)_ – anti‑discrimination.
    *   _Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)_.
*   Regulatory Authority: U.S. Department of Labor (DOL), Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC), OSHA.
*   Applicability: Federal law sets minimum standards; states may impose stricter rules (e.g., California, New York).

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   At‑will employment (default).
    *   Fixed‑term contracts (rare, usually for executives).
    *   Unionized employment under CBAs.
*   Probationary Periods: Not federally regulated; employer discretion.
*   Mandatory Clauses: Not required by law, but best practice includes wages, hours, benefits, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: English standard; bilingual contracts allowed.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week under FLSA.
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → hours beyond 40/week (non‑exempt employees).
*   Rest Periods: Not federally mandated; state laws vary (e.g., California requires meal/rest breaks).

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave (Vacation): No federal mandate; employer policy or state law.
*   Public Holidays: 11 federal holidays (e.g., Independence Day, Thanksgiving, Christmas); not mandatory for private employers.
*   Sick Leave: No federal mandate; some states (e.g., California, New York) require paid sick leave.
*   FMLA Leave: Up to 12 weeks unpaid, job‑protected leave for family/medical reasons (applies to employers with ≥50 employees).
*   Maternity/Paternity Leave: Covered under FMLA (unpaid); states may offer paid programs (e.g., California, New Jersey).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Federal Minimum Wage: $7.25/hour (unchanged since 2009).
*   State Minimum Wages: Higher in many states (e.g., California ~$16/hour in 2025).
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social Security (employer 6.2%, employee 6.2%).
    *   Medicare (employer 1.45%, employee 1.45%).
    *   Unemployment insurance (state‑specific).
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in practice.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   At‑Will Employment: Employers may terminate without cause (except for discrimination, retaliation, or protected reasons).
*   Notice Periods: No federal requirement; immediate termination allowed.
*   Severance Pay: Not statutory; provided by employer policy or CBAs.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation (if required by employer policy).
*   Employees must contribute to Social Security and Medicare.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   DOL: Oversees wage/hour compliance.
*   EEOC: Enforces anti‑discrimination laws.
*   OSHA: Enforces workplace safety.
*   State Labour Agencies: Enforce state‑specific rules.
*   CBAs: Binding in unionized workplaces.
