**Country Name: Argentina**

**🇦🇷 Argentina – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Contract Law No. 20,744 (1976, amended)_ – contracts, rights, termination.
    *   _Law No. 24,013_ – employment promotion and unemployment insurance.
    *   _Law No. 19,587_ – occupational health and safety.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour, Employment and Social Security.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs are highly influential and often override statutory minimums.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (default).
    *   Fixed‑term (must be justified, max 5 years).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/seasonal work.
*   Probationary Periods: 3 months (permanent contracts).
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Spanish mandatory.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 48 hours/week (8 hours/day, 6 days).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → weekdays.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → weekends/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 12 hours daily rest; 35 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave (based on tenure):
    *   14 days (<5 Years of Service)
    *   21 days (5–10 years).
    *   28 days (10–20 years).
    *   35 days (≥20 years).
*   Public Holidays: 15 annually (e.g., Independence Day, May Revolution Day, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave:
    *   3 months (if <5 years of Service)
    *   6 months (if ≥5 years service).
*   Maternity Leave: 90 days (45 before, 45 after birth, paid).
*   Paternity Leave: 2 days (paid).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage (Salario Mínimo Vital y Móvil): Adjusted periodically (~ARS 156,000/month in 2025).
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (employer ~23%, employee ~17%).
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay (Aguinaldo): Mandatory — paid in June and December.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   15 days (probation).
    *   1 month (< 5 Years service)
    *   2 months (≥5 years service).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   1 month’s salary per year of service (based on highest monthly salary).
    *   Minimum entitlement: 1 month’s salary.
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Ministry of Labour: Oversees compliance.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.
