**Country Name: Canada**

**🇨🇦 Canada – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Canada Labour Code_ – applies to federally regulated industries (banking, telecom, transport).
    *   _Provincial/Territorial Employment Standards Acts_ – govern most employees (e.g., Ontario ESA, BC ESA).
    *   _Human Rights Acts_ – prohibit discrimination.
    *   _Occupational Health and Safety Acts_.
*   Regulatory Authority:
    *   Federal: Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC).
    *   Provincial: Ministries of Labour (e.g., Ontario Ministry of Labour).
*   Applicability: Most employees fall under provincial law; federal law covers ~10% of workforce.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (allowed if justified).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/seasonal work.
*   Probationary Periods: Typically 3 months (varies by province).
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: English or French depending on jurisdiction.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 8 hours/day, 40 hours/week (federal); provinces vary (e.g., Ontario 44 hours/week).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → hours beyond statutory weekly limit.
*   Rest Periods: Minimum daily and weekly rest vary by province.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave (Vacation):
    *   2 weeks/year (after 1 year of service).
    *   3 weeks/year (after 5 years).
    *   4 weeks/year (after 10 years in some provinces).
*   Public Holidays: 9–12 annually depending on province (e.g., Canada Day, Labour Day, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave:
    *   Federal: 10 paid sick days/year (introduced in 2022).
    *   Provinces vary (e.g., Ontario: 3 unpaid sick days).
*   Maternity Leave: 15–17 weeks (unpaid, but EI maternity benefits available).
*   Parental Leave: Up to 63 weeks shared (EI parental benefits available).
*   Paternity Leave: Covered under parental leave.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Varies by province (e.g., Ontario ~CAD 16.55/hour in 2025).
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Canada Pension Plan (CPP) or Quebec Pension Plan (QPP).
    *   Employment Insurance (EI).
    *   Employer health taxes (province‑specific).
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in practice.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods (Employment Standards):
    *   1 week (< 1 years of Service)
    *   2 weeks (1–3 years).
    *   3 weeks (3–4 years).
    *   Up to 8 weeks (≥8 years).
*   Severance Pay (Ontario example):
    *   Required if employee has ≥5 years service and employer payroll ≥CAD 2.5M.
    *   Formula: 1 week’s pay per year of service (max 26 weeks).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation (usually 2 weeks).
*   Employees must contribute to CPP/QPP and EI.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Federal Labour Program (ESDC): Oversees federally regulated employers.
*   Provincial Ministries of Labour: Enforce employment standards.
*   Labour Boards & Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across unionized workplaces.

**9\. References**

*   Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC): https://www.canada.ca/en/employment-social-development (canada.ca in Bing)

**🇨🇦 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Canada Labour Code**: Governs federally regulated industries.
*   **Provincial Employment Standards Acts**: Govern most employees (e.g., Ontario ESA, BC ESA).
*   **Human Rights Acts**: Prohibit discrimination.
*   **Occupational Health and Safety Acts**: Workplace safety standards.
*   **Language Requirement**: Contracts must be in English or French depending on jurisdiction.
