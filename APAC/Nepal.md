**Country Name: Nepal**

**🇳🇵 Nepal – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Act, 2017 (2074)_ – contracts, rights, termination.
    *   _Labour Regulations, 2018_.
    *   _Social Security Act, 2017_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour, Employment and Social Security.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.
*   Recent Developments (2025–26):
*   Expansion of social security coverage.
*   Minimum wage adjustments.
*   Strengthening of occupational safety standards.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (indefinite).
    *   Fixed‑term (allowed if justified).
    *   Casual/seasonal work.
    *   Apprenticeship contracts.
*   Probationary Periods: Max 6 months.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Nepali mandatory.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week.
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime.
*   Rest Periods: Weekly rest day (usually Saturday).

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   18 days/year (paid).
*   Public Holidays: ~13 annually (including Dashain, Tihar, Holi, Constitution Day).
*   Sick Leave: 12 days/year (paid).
*   Maternity Leave: 98 days (paid, up to 2 surviving children).
*   Paternity Leave: 15 days (paid).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted periodically (~NPR 15,000/month in 2025).
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social Security Fund (SSF): employer 11%, employee 10%.
    *   Covers pension, health, accident, maternity, unemployment.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Festival bonus (mandatory, equivalent to one month’s salary).

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   30 days (permanent employees).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   30 days’ wages per year of service (redundancy/unfair dismissal).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation (usually 30 days).
*   Employees must contribute to SSF.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate: Oversees compliance.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.

**9\. References**

*   Labour Act, 2017 (Nepal) – official legislation.
*   Ministry of Labour, Employment and Social Security (Nepal) – official portal.
*   ILO Nepal country reports – labour standards and compliance.
