**Country Name: Australia**

**🇦🇺 Australia – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

**•** Primary Laws:

• Fair Work Act 2009 – core employment law.

• National Employment Standards (NES) – minimum entitlements.

• Work Health and Safety Acts (federal and state).

• Superannuation Guarantee (Administration) Act 1992.

• Regulatory Authority: Fair Work Commission (FWC), Fair Work Ombudsman (FWO).

• Applicability: Covers most employees nationally, with some state‑specific variations.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

**•** Types of Employment:

• Permanent (full‑time, part‑time).

• Fixed‑term.

• Casual (with casual loading).

• Probationary Periods: Common practice 3–6 months; no statutory maximum, but unfair dismissal protections apply after 6 months (12 months for small businesses).

• Mandatory Clauses: job scope, wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.

• Language Requirement: English; bilingual contracts allowed.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

**•** Standard Hours (NES): 38 hours/week + reasonable additional hours.

• Overtime Rules:

• Rates vary by award/enterprise agreement.

• Commonly 1.5× for first 2–3 hours, 2× thereafter.

• Rest Breaks: Entitlements vary by award.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

**•** Annual Leave (NES):

• 4 weeks/year (pro‑rata for part‑time).

• 5 weeks/year for shift workers.

• Public Holidays: 10–12 annually (national + state).

• Personal/Carer’s Leave: 10 days/year (accrued).

• Compassionate Leave: 2 days per occasion.

• Parental Leave: Up to 12 months unpaid (with option to request extension to 24 months).

• Paid Parental Leave Scheme: Government provides up to 20 weeks at national minimum wage.

• Long Service Leave: Varies by state (typically 8.67 weeks after 10 years).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

**•** Minimum Wage: Set annually by Fair Work Commission; ~AUD 24/hour (2025).

• Overtime Pay: Award/enterprise agreement specific.

• Mandatory Contributions:

• Superannuation Guarantee (11% of ordinary earnings, rising to 12% by 2025).

• Penalty Rates: Higher pay for weekends, public holidays, night shifts (award‑based).

**6\. Termination & Severance**

**•** Notice Period (NES):

1 week (<1 year service).

2 weeks (1–3 years).

3 weeks (3–5 years).

4 weeks (≥5 years).

+1 week if employee ≥45 years old with ≥2 years service.

• Redundancy Pay (NES):

4 weeks (1–2 years service).

Scales up to 16 weeks (≥10 years service).

• Exemptions: Small businesses (<15 employees) exempt from redundancy pay**.**

**7\. Employee Obligations**

**•** Must comply with company policies and statutory obligations.

• Must give notice for resignation (same as employer notice periods).

• Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without redundancy pay.

• Employees must contribute to superannuation if self‑employed; otherwise employer contributes.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

**•** Fair Work Ombudsman: Investigates complaints, enforces compliance.

• Fair Work Commission: Handles disputes, unfair dismissal claims.

• Penalties: Fines for breaches of NES, awards, agreements.

• Work Health & Safety Regulators: State agencies enforce safety laws.

**🇦🇺 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **National Employment Standards (NES)**:
    *   11 minimum entitlements (hours, leave, notice, redundancy, flexible work requests).
*   **Awards & Enterprise Agreements**:
    *   Industry‑specific rules on pay, overtime, penalty rates, allowances.
    *   Awards cover most employees unless enterprise agreements apply.
*   **Casual Employment**:
    *   Casuals receive a loading (usually 25%) instead of leave entitlements.
    *   Right to request conversion to permanent after 12 months (if regular and ongoing).
*   **Unfair Dismissal Protections**:
    *   Apply after 6 months service (12 months for small businesses).
    *   Dismissals must be “harsh, unjust, or unreasonable” to be challenged.
*   **Work Health & Safety (WHS)**:
    *   Duty of care for employers to provide safe workplaces.
    *   Enforced by state regulators (e.g., SafeWork NSW, WorkSafe Victoria).
*   **Recent Developments**:
*   Superannuation guarantee increased to 11% (2024), rising to 12% by 2025.
*   Ongoing reforms around casual employment definitions and gig economy protections.
*   Enhanced protections for flexible work requests (parents, carers, older workers).
