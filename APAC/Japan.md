**Country Name: Japan**

**🇯🇵 Japan – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Standards Act (LSA)_ – core employment law.
    *   _Labour Contract Act_.
    *   _Industrial Safety and Health Act_.
    *   _Employment Security Act_.
    *   _Social Insurance Laws_ (Health Insurance, Pension, Employment Insurance, Workers’ Compensation).
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare (MHLW).
*   Applicability: Covers all employees, with some exemptions for senior management.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Regular (permanent).
    *   Fixed‑term (must not exceed 3 years, except for highly specialized roles).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Dispatch/agency workers (regulated under Worker Dispatching Act).
*   Probationary Periods: Common practice 3–6 months; no statutory maximum, but courts generally uphold ≤1 year.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, working hours, holidays, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Japanese; bilingual contracts allowed but Japanese version prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours (LSA): 8 hours/day, 40 hours/week.
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.25×_ hourly wage → overtime on normal working days.
    *   _1.35×_ hourly wage → overtime exceeding 60 hours/month.
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → work on rest days.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → work on statutory holidays.
*   Recent Reform (Work Style Reform, 2019):
*   Overtime capped at 45 hours/month and 360 hours/year (exceptions for special circumstances).

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave (LSA Article 39):
    *   6 months service → 10 days.
    *   Increases with tenure up to 20 days after 6.5 years.
    *   Employers must ensure at least 5 days are taken annually.
*   Public Holidays: 16 national holidays (e.g., New Year’s Day, Constitution Day).
*   Sick Leave: Not statutory; usually covered by company policy or social insurance.
*   Maternity Leave: 14 weeks (6 before, 8 after childbirth).
*   Childcare Leave: Up to 1 year (extendable to 2 years in certain cases).
*   Paternity Leave: Expanded in 2022 → up to 4 weeks within 8 weeks of childbirth.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Set by prefecture; national average ~¥1,000/hour (2025).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Health Insurance.
    *   Pension (Employees’ Pension Insurance).
    *   Employment Insurance.
    *   Workers’ Compensation Insurance.
*   Bonus: Not statutory, but common practice (summer and winter bonuses).

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Period: 30 days (or payment in lieu).
*   Grounds for Termination:
    *   Economic redundancy.
    *   Misconduct.
    *   Incompetence.
*   Strict Judicial Standard: Dismissals must be “objectively reasonable and socially acceptable.”
*   Severance Pay: Not mandated by law, but widely provided by company policy or collective agreements.
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for serious misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give 30 days’ notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal.
*   Employees must contribute to social insurance schemes.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Standards Inspection Offices: Monitor compliance with LSA.
*   Penalties: Fines or imprisonment for violations (e.g., unpaid wages, excessive overtime).
*   Dispute Resolution: Labour Tribunal → courts.
*   Recent Note: Work Style Reform Act strengthened enforcement of overtime caps.

**9\. References**

*   Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare (MHLW): [https://www.mhlw.go.jp](https://www.mhlw.go.jp/)
*   Labour Standards Act (LSA) – Japanese Law Translation.
*   Work Style Reform Act (2019) – MHLW guidance.

**🇯🇵 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Labour Standards Act (Articles 32–39):**
    *   Defines working hours, overtime, and annual leave entitlements.
*   **Labour Contract Act (Article 16):**
    *   Dismissals must be “objectively reasonable and socially acceptable.”
*   **Work Style Reform Act (2019):**
    *   Introduced overtime caps and mandatory annual leave usage (at least 5 days).
*   **Dispatch Workers (Worker Dispatching Act):**
    *   Regulates agency workers, maximum dispatch periods, and equal treatment provisions.
*   **Social Insurance:**
    *   Mandatory enrollment in health, pension, employment, and workers’ compensation insurance.
*   **Regional Variations:**
    *   Minimum wage differs by prefecture (Tokyo highest, Okinawa lowest).
*   **Recent Developments:**
*   Paternity leave expanded in 2022.
*   Ongoing government push for “work‑life balance” and reduction of excessive overtime.
