**Country Name: Indonesia**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   **Primary Laws**:
    *   _Manpower Law No. 13 of 2003_ (as amended by _Job Creation Law No. 11 of 2020_).
    *   _Social Security Laws_ (BPJS Kesehatan – health, BPJS Ketenagakerjaan – employment security).
*   **Regulatory Authority**: Ministry of Manpower (Kementerian Ketenagakerjaan).
*   **Applicability Thresholds**: Applies to all employers regardless of size.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   **Mandatory Clauses**: job title, wages, working hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   **Language Requirement**: Must be in Bahasa Indonesia (foreign language contracts are invalid if challenged).
*   **Contract Types**: Permanent (PKWTT), Fixed‑term (PKWT), Outsourced/Agency.
*   **Rare Note**: PKWT contracts must be registered with the Ministry of Manpower.

**3\. Working Hours & Rest**

*   **Standard Hours**: 40 hours/week (7 hours/day for 6‑day week OR 8 hours/day for 5‑day week).
*   **Overtime**: Max 3 hours/day, 14 hours/week; paid at 1.5x–2x hourly wage.
*   **Rest Periods**: 30‑minute break after 4 consecutive hours; 1 weekly rest day.
*   **Rare Note**: Certain sectors (e.g., oil & gas, transport) have special working hour rules.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   **Annual Leave**: 12 working days after 12 months of continuous service.
*   **Public Holidays**: 15–16 days annually (including religious holidays).
*   **Sick Leave**: Paid, with medical certificate; pay reduces after 4 months (per Article 93).
*   **Maternity Leave**: 3 months (1.5 before + 1.5 after childbirth); employer pays full wages.
*   **Paternity Leave**: 2 days for childbirth, 2 days for marriage, 1–2 days for bereavement.
*   **Rare Note**: Employees are entitled to long service leave (cuti panjang) of 2 months after 6 years, plus 1 month every subsequent 3 years, if company policy allows.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   **Minimum Wage**: Region‑based (UMR/UMP), updated annually.
*   **Overtime Pay**: Statutory multipliers apply.
*   **Mandatory Bonus**: Religious Holiday Allowance (THR) – 1 month’s salary, payable before major religious holiday.
*   **Social Security Contributions**:
    *   BPJS Kesehatan (health insurance).
    *   BPJS Ketenagakerjaan (work accident, death, old age, pension, unemployment).
*   **Rare Note**: Employers must register all employees with BPJS, even short‑term hires.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   **Notice Period**: Generally 30 days, depending on contract type.
*   **Grounds for Termination**: redundancy, misconduct, resignation, retirement.
*   **Severance Pay**: Formula based on years of service (Article 156 of Manpower Law). Includes severance, service appreciation, and compensation rights.
*   **Rare Note**: Court/labor office approval may be required for certain dismissals.

**7\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   **Labor Inspections**: Conducted by Ministry of Manpower.
*   **Penalties**: Fines, imprisonment, or revocation of business license for non‑compliance.
*   **Dispute Resolution**: Bipartite negotiation → mediation/arbitration → Industrial Relations Court.
*   **Rare Note**: Employees can file anonymous complaints with BPJS or labor office.

**8\. Practical HR Guidance**

*   **Best Practices**:
    *   Draft bilingual contracts but ensure Bahasa Indonesia version prevails.
    *   Align payroll with regional minimum wage updates.
    *   Budget for THR bonus annually.
*   **Common Claims**: THR disputes, severance calculation errors, overtime underpayment.
*   **Risk Mitigation**: Maintain compliance records, register all employees with BPJS, document leave approvals.

**9\. References**

*   **Manpower Law No. 13/2003** (as amended by Job Creation Law No. 11/2020).
*   **Article 93** – Sick leave pay reduction rules.
*   **Article 156** – Severance pay formula.
*   **BPJS Laws** – Social security obligations.
*   **Official Source**: Indonesia Manpower Law – English summary (KLRI equivalent) (bing.com in Bing)  
      
    **Further In-depth Explanations:  
      
    **Employment Contracts
*   **Types of Contracts**:
*   **PKWTT (Permanent contract)**: No fixed end date; termination requires valid grounds and severance.
*   **PKWT (Fixed‑term contract)**: Limited duration (max 5 years after Job Creation Law reform). Must be in writing, in Bahasa Indonesia, and registered with the Ministry of Manpower.
*   **Outsourcing/Agency contracts**: Allowed only for non‑core activities.  
    

**Exemptions/Notes**:

*   PKWT cannot be used for permanent/core jobs.
*   If PKWT is improperly applied, it is deemed a PKWTT.

  
**Working Hours & Overtime**

*   **Standard Hours**:
    *   7 hours/day, 6 days/week OR 8 hours/day, 5 days/week (total 40 hours/week).
*   **Overtime Rules**:
    *   Max 3 hours/day, 14 hours/week.
    *   **Multiplier**:
        *   _1.5x_ hourly wage → for the **first hour** of overtime.
        *   _2x_ hourly wage → for **subsequent hours**.
    *   **Special Cases**:
    *   Public holiday work: 2x for first 7–8 hours, 3x for next hour, 4x for hours beyond that.
*   **Exemptions**: Certain managerial/executive roles may be excluded from overtime entitlement.  
    

**Leave & Holidays**

*   **Annual Leave**:
    *   12 working days after 12 months of continuous service.
    *   Does not increase with tenure under statute, but some companies grant additional leave contractually.
*   **Public Holidays**: 15–16 days annually (national + religious).
*   **Sick Leave**:
    *   Paid leave with medical certificate.
    *   Wage entitlement reduces after 4 months:
    *   Months 1–4: 100% wages.
    *   Months 5–8: 75%.
    *   Months 9–12: 50%.
    *   Beyond 12 months: 25%.
*   **Maternity Leave**: 3 months (1.5 before + 1.5 after childbirth).
*   **Paternity Leave**: 2 days for childbirth, 2 days for marriage, 1–2 days for bereavement.
*   **Rare Benefit**: _Long Service Leave (Cuti Panjang)_ – 2 months after 6 years, plus 1 month every 3 years thereafter, if company policy allows.

**When Severance is Mandatory:**

*   **Article 156 of Manpower Law No. 13/2003 (as amended by Job Creation Law No. 11/2020)** governs severance.
*   Severance is **mandatory** when:
    *   Employer terminates a **PKWTT (permanent contract)** employee for redundancy, efficiency, or other valid grounds.
    *   Employer terminates a **PKWT (fixed‑term contract)** employee **before contract expiry** (compensation owed for remaining term).
    *   Termination occurs due to company closure, bankruptcy, or merger.
*   Severance is **not owed** if:
*   Employee resigns voluntarily.
*   Employee is terminated during probation (PKWTT probation max 3 months).
*   Employee is dismissed for serious misconduct (fraud, theft, assault, etc.), though compensation rights may still apply.

**Service Thresholds:**

*   **Eligibility**: Severance applies after **12 months of continuous service**.
*   **Formula (Article 156)**:
*   **Severance Pay**: Based on years of service.
    *   1–2 years → 2 months’ wages.
    *   2–3 years → 3 months’ wages.
    *   … up to 9+ years → 9 months’ wages.
*   **Service Appreciation Pay**: Additional months of wages for longer tenure (starts after 3 years).
*   **Compensation Rights**: Payment for unused leave, relocation costs, housing/medical allowance (15% of severance + appreciation).

  
**Contract Type Differences:**

*   **PKWTT (Permanent)**: Full severance entitlement applies if terminated after 12 months.
*   **PKWT (Fixed‑term)**:
    *   If contract ends naturally → no severance.
    *   If terminated early → compensation equal to wages for remaining contract period.
*   **Probationary PKWTT**: No severance if terminated during probation.

**Employee Obligations:**

*   **Resignation**: Employee must give 30 days’ notice; severance not owed, but compensation rights (unused leave, relocation allowance) may apply.
*   **Misconduct**: Employee may lose severance entitlement but employer must prove misconduct under Article 158.
*   **BPJS Registration**: Employees must participate in BPJS programs (health + employment security).
*   **Leave Usage**: Employees must comply with leave request procedures; unused leave is compensated at termination.

**Scenarios:**

*   **Resignation after 2 years** → No severance, but unused leave encashment + THR if resignation occurs before religious holiday.
*   **Termination for redundancy after 5 years** → Severance (5 months), service appreciation (2 months), compensation rights (15%).
*   **PKWT terminated 6 months early** → Compensation equal to 6 months’ wages.
*   **Dismissal for misconduct after 3 years** → Severance may be forfeited, but compensation rights (unused leave, relocation allowance) still owed.
