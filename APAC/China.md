**Country Name: China**

**🇨🇳 China – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labor Law of the People’s Republic of China (1995)_.
    *   _Labor Contract Law (2008, amended 2012)_.
    *   _Social Insurance Law (2011)_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security (MOHRSS).
*   Applicability: Applies to all employers and employees in China, including foreign‑invested enterprises.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Contracts:
    *   Fixed‑term – most common; must specify duration.
    *   Open‑ended (indefinite) – required after two consecutive fixed‑term renewals or 10 years of service.
    *   Project‑based – tied to completion of a specific project.
*   **Probationary Periods:**
    *   ≤1 year contract → max 1 month probation.
    *   1–3 year contract → max 2 months probation.
    *   ≥3 year or open‑ended contract → max 6 months probation.
*   **Mandatory Clauses: job duties, workplace, wages, working hours, social insurance, termination conditions.**
*   **Language Requirement: Must be in Chinese; bilingual contracts allowed but Chinese version prevails.**

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 8 hours/day, 44 hours/week (Labor Law Article 36).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime on regular working days.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → overtime on rest days (if no compensatory time off).
    *   _3×_ hourly wage → overtime on statutory holidays.
*   Special Working Hour Systems:
*   Flexible or comprehensive working hour systems allowed with government approval (e.g., for transport, construction).

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave (Regulations on Paid Annual Leave, 2008):
    *   1–10 years service → 5 days.
    *   10–20 years service → 10 days.
    *   20+ years service → 15 days.
*   Public Holidays: 11 national holidays (Spring Festival, National Day, etc.).
*   Sick Leave: Paid, with rate depending on tenure and local rules (60–100% of wages).
*   Maternity Leave: 98 days minimum; extended in some provinces (e.g., +30 days).
*   Paternity Leave: 7–15 days, depending on province.
*   Other Leave: Marriage leave (3 days), funeral leave (1–3 days).  
    

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Set by local governments; varies by province/city.
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Social Insurance Contributions:
    *   Pension.
    *   Medical insurance.
    *   Unemployment insurance.
    *   Work injury insurance.
    *   Maternity insurance.
*   Housing Fund: Mandatory employer and employee contributions.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Period: 30 days (or payment in lieu).
*   Grounds for Termination:
    *   Mutual agreement.
    *   Expiry of contract.
    *   Employee misconduct or incompetence.
    *   Economic redundancy.
*   Severance Pay:
    *   Mandatory if employer terminates without employee fault.
    *   Formula: 1 month’s average wages per year of service (capped at 12 months).
*   Exemptions: No severance if employee resigns voluntarily or is dismissed for serious misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and labor discipline.
*   Must give 30 days’ notice for resignation (or 3 days during probation).
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence, serious negligence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to social insurance and housing fund.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labor Inspections: Conducted by MOHRSS and local labor bureaus.
*   Penalties: Fines, compensation orders, or criminal liability for violations.
*   Dispute Resolution: Labor arbitration → People’s Court.
*   Rare Note: Employees can file anonymous complaints with local labor bureaus.

**9\. References**

*   Labor Law of the PRC (1995) – National People’s Congress.
*   Labor Contract Law (2008, amended 2012) – Standing Committee of the NPC.
*   Social Insurance Law (2011) – NPC.

🇨🇳 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know

*   **Labor Contract Law (Articles 14–20)**: Rules on open‑ended contracts after two renewals or 10 years service.
*   **Article 36 Labor Law**: Standard working hours (8/day, 44/week).
*   **Article 44 Labor Law**: Overtime multipliers (1.5×, 2×, 3×).
*   **Paid Annual Leave Regulations (2008)**: Leave entitlement based on cumulative service across employers.
*   **Social Insurance Law (Articles 10–15)**: Employer obligations for pension, medical, unemployment, work injury, maternity.
*   **Housing Fund Regulations**: Mandatory contributions vary by city/province.
*   **Termination Protections**:
    *   Pregnant employees, employees on medical leave, and employees injured at work cannot be terminated except under special circumstances.
    *   Severance capped at 12 months wages, but local rules may add protections.
*   **Regional Variations**: Provinces may extend maternity/paternity leave (e.g., Beijing, Shanghai).
