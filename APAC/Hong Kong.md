**🇭🇰 Hong Kong – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Employment Ordinance (Cap. 57)_ – core employment law.
    *   _Minimum Wage Ordinance (Cap. 608)_.
    *   _Employees’ Compensation Ordinance (Cap. 282)_.
    *   _Mandatory Provident Fund Schemes Ordinance (Cap. 485)_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Labour Department of Hong Kong SAR.
*   Applicability: Covers most employees, with exceptions for civil servants, seafarers, and apprentices under special rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Contracts:
    *   Continuous contracts (≥4 weeks, ≥18 hours/week) → full statutory benefits.
    *   Fixed‑term contracts allowed.
*   Probationary Periods: Not legally capped, but common practice is 3 months.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, working hours, holidays, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: English or Chinese; bilingual contracts common.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: No statutory maximum working hours.
*   Overtime Rules: No statutory overtime pay requirement unless contractually agreed.
*   Rest Days: At least 1 rest day in every 7 days for continuous contracts.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave (Employment Ordinance):
    *   1 year service → 7 days.
    *   Increases with tenure up to 14 days after 9 years.
*   Statutory Holidays: 13 days annually (e.g., Lunar New Year, National Day).
*   Sick Leave:
    *   Accrues at 2 days/month (first year), then 4 days/month.
    *   Max accumulation: 120 days.
    *   Paid at 80% of wages if supported by medical certificate.
*   Maternity Leave: 14 weeks (extended from 10 weeks in 2020). Employer pays 10 weeks, government reimburses 4 weeks (capped).
*   Paternity Leave: 5 days.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: HKD 40/hour (2025 adjustment).
*   Mandatory Provident Fund (MPF): Employer and employee contributions (5% each, capped).
*   Employees’ Compensation: Mandatory insurance for work injuries.
*   End‑of‑Year Payment: Common practice (13th month), but not statutory unless contractually agreed.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Period:
    *   During probation: 7 days (after first month).
    *   After probation: 1 month or as agreed.
*   Severance Pay:
    *   Eligible if ≥24 months continuous service and dismissed for redundancy.
    *   Formula: 2/3 × last month’s wages × years of service (capped).
*   Long Service Payment:
    *   Eligible if ≥5 years service and dismissed for reasons other than misconduct.
    *   Same formula as severance.
*   Exemptions: No severance/long service if employee resigns voluntarily or is dismissed for serious misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give notice for resignation (usually 1 month).
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance/long service payment.
*   Employees must contribute to MPF unless exempt (e.g., expatriates with overseas retirement schemes).

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Department inspections: Contracts, wages, leave records.
*   Penalties: Fines or imprisonment for violations (e.g., failure to pay wages, MPF non‑compliance).
*   Dispute Resolution: Labour Tribunal → courts.

**🇭🇰 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Employment Ordinance (Cap. 57)**:
    *   Defines “continuous contract” (≥4 weeks, ≥18 hours/week) as the threshold for statutory benefits.
    *   Sets rules for wage payment, maternity protection, termination procedures.
*   **Severance vs Long Service Payment**:
    *   Mutually exclusive — employees cannot claim both.
    *   Severance applies to redundancy; long service applies to dismissal after ≥5 years service (except misconduct).
*   **Rest Days & Holidays**:
    *   Employers must grant at least 1 rest day in every 7 days.
    *   Statutory holidays are fixed; employers cannot substitute with other days unless agreed.
*   **Sick Leave Protection**:
    *   Employees cannot be dismissed while on paid statutory sick leave.
*   **MPF Exemptions**:
    *   Expatriates covered by overseas retirement schemes, domestic helpers, and short‑term employees may be exempt.
*   **Recent Developments**:
*   Minimum wage adjusted to HKD 40/hour (2025).
*   Maternity leave extended to 14 weeks (2020 reform, still current).
*   Ongoing discussions about expanding statutory holidays to align with general holidays (would increase from 13 to 17).
