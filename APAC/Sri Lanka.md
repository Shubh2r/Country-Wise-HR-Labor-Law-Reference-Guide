**Country Name: Sri Lanka**

**🇱🇰 Sri Lanka – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Shop and Office Employees Act_ – working hours, leave, holidays.
    *   _Factories Ordinance_ – workplace safety.
    *   _Wages Boards Ordinance_ – minimum wages by sector.
    *   _Industrial Disputes Act_ – dispute resolution, union rights.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour and Foreign Employment.
*   Recent Developments (2026):
*   National minimum wage increase.
*   New wage support and inclusion initiatives for differently‑abled employees.
*   Contributory pension scheme for Sri Lankan expatriate workers.
*   Accelerated digitalisation of payroll and tax compliance systems.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (indefinite).
    *   Fixed‑term (allowed if justified).
    *   Casual/temporary work.
*   Probationary Periods: Typically 6 months.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Sinhala, Tamil, or English depending on context.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 8 hours/day, 45 hours/week (Shop and Office Employees Act).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime.
*   Rest Periods: Weekly rest day (usually Sunday).

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   14 days/year after 1 year of service.
*   Public Holidays: ~25 annually (including Poya days, Independence Day, Sinhala & Tamil New Year, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: 7 days/year (paid).
*   Maternity Leave: 84 working days (paid).
*   Paternity Leave: Not statutory, but some employers provide 3–5 days.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted periodically by Wages Boards; increased under 2026 budget.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Employees’ Provident Fund (EPF): employer 12%, employee 8%.
    *   Employees’ Trust Fund (ETF): employer 3%.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in practice.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   1 month typical for permanent employees.
*   Severance Pay:
    *   Formula under Termination of Employment of Workmen Act (TEWA): based on years of service and last drawn salary.
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation (usually 1 month).
*   Employees must contribute to EPF.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate: Oversees compliance.
*   Labour Tribunals: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding in unionised workplaces.
