**Country Name: Malaysia**

**🇲🇾 Malaysia – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Employment Act 1955_ (core employment law, applies to Peninsular Malaysia).
    *   _Labour Ordinances_ (Sabah & Sarawak).
    *   _Industrial Relations Act 1967_ (trade unions, disputes).
    *   _Employees’ Provident Fund Act 1991_.
    *   _Employees’ Social Security Act 1969 (SOCSO)_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Human Resources (MOHR).
*   Applicability: Employment Act applies mainly to employees earning ≤RM 2,000/month or in specified categories (manual workers, etc.), but many provisions are extended by contract to higher‑paid staff.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent.
    *   Fixed‑term (must specify duration; renewal beyond 2–3 cycles may be deemed permanent).
    *   Probationary (common practice: 3–6 months).
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, holidays, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: English or Malay; bilingual contracts common.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week.
*   Overtime Rules (Employment Act):
    *   _1.5×_ hourly rate → overtime on normal working days.
    *   _2×_ hourly rate → work on rest days.
    *   _3×_ hourly rate → work on public holidays.
*   Rest Days: At least 1 day off per week.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave (Employment Act):
    *   1–2 years service → 8 days.
    *   2–5 years service → 12 days.
    *   ≥5 years service → 16 days.
*   Public Holidays: 11 gazetted days + additional state holidays.
*   Sick Leave:
    *   2–5 years service → 18 days/year.
    *   ≥5 years service → 22 days/year.
    *   Hospitalization → up to 60 days/year.
*   Maternity Leave: 98 days (extended in 2022).
*   Paternity Leave: 7 days (introduced in 2023).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: RM 1,500/month (2023 update; subject to review).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Employees’ Provident Fund (EPF).
    *   Social Security Organization (SOCSO).
    *   Employment Insurance System (EIS).
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common practice.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Period (Employment Act):
    *   2–5 years service → 6 weeks.
    *   ≥5 years service → 8 weeks.
*   Severance/Termination Benefits:
    *   10 days wages per year of service (
    *   15 days wages per year of service (2–5 years).
    *   20 days wages per year of service (≥5 years).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct, resignation, or retirement.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give notice for resignation (same as employer notice periods).
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to EPF, SOCSO, and EIS.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Department inspections: Contracts, wages, leave records.
*   Penalties: Fines or imprisonment for violations.
*   Dispute Resolution: Industrial Relations Department → Industrial Court.

**🇲🇾 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Employment Act coverage:**
    *   Applies mainly to employees earning ≤RM 2,000/month or in specified categories (manual workers, etc.).
    *   Higher‑paid employees are covered by contract terms, but many employers extend statutory benefits to all staff.
*   **Fixed‑term contracts:**
    *   Renewals beyond 2–3 cycles may be deemed permanent employment by courts.
*   **Termination Benefits (Regulation 1980):**
    *   Detailed formulas for retrenchment, redundancy, and closure.
*   **Industrial Relations Act, 1967:**
    *   Governs collective bargaining, union rights, and dispute resolution.
*   **Occupational Safety and Health Act, 1994:**
    *   Employer obligations for workplace safety and health.
*   **Recent Developments:**
*   Maternity leave extended to 98 days (2022).
*   Paternity leave introduced (7 days, 2023).
*   Minimum wage raised to RM 1,500/month (2023).
*   Ongoing discussions about expanding Employment Act coverage to all employees regardless of salary.
