**Country Name: Thailand**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   **Primary Law:** Labor Protection Act (LPA) B.E. 2541 (1998), with amendments (latest No. 9 in 2025).
*   **Regulatory Authority:** Ministry of Labour.
*   **Applicability:** Applies to all employers regardless of size.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   **Types of Contracts:**
    *   **Indefinite (Permanent) –** default employment relationship.
    *   **Fixed‑term** – allowed only for specific projects or seasonal work; must be in writing.
    *   **Probationary –** max 119 days; termination during probation requires wage payment until last day.
*   **Language Requirement:** Thai language preferred; bilingual contracts common.
*   **Exemptions:** Fixed‑term contracts cannot be used to avoid severance obligations if work is continuous.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   **Standard Hours:** 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week (or 40 hours/week for hazardous work).
*   **Overtime Rules:**
    *   **Max 36 hours/month.**
    *   **Multiplier:**
    *   **_1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime on normal working days.**
    *   **_2×_ hourly wage → overtime on holidays.**
    *   **_3×_ hourly wage → overtime on holidays beyond normal working hours.**
*   **Rest Periods:** At least 1 hour break after 5 consecutive hours.
*   **Weekly Rest:** At least 1 day off per week.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   **Annual Leave:**
    *   Minimum 6 working days after 1 year of service.
    *   Employers may grant more based on tenure.
*   **Public Holidays:** At least 13 days annually (including Constitution Day, King’s Birthday, etc.).
*   **Sick Leave:** 30 days/year with full pay; medical certificate required if >3 days.
*   **Maternity Leave:** 98 days (extended in 2024); 45 days employer‑paid, remainder covered by social security.
*   **Paternity Leave**: 15 days (introduced in 2025 amendment).
*   **Other Leave:** Military service leave, training leave, sterilization leave.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   **Minimum Wage:** Province‑based, THB 337–400/day (Bangkok THB 400 as of July 2025).
*   **Overtime Pay:** As above.
*   **Holiday Pay:** Double wages if employee works on public holiday.
*   **Social Security Contributions:**
    *   Employer: 5% of wages (capped).
    *   Employee: 5% of wages (capped).
    *   Covers medical, unemployment, child allowance, pension.
*   **Welfare Fund:** Mandatory contributions (from Oct 2025) to enhance severance benefits.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   **Notice Period:** At least 1 pay cycle (usually 30 days).
*   **Severance Pay (mandatory after 120 days service):**
    *   120 days– < 1 year : 30 Day’s Wages
    *   1–< 3 Years : 90 Days Wages
    *   3–< 6 Years : 180 Days Wages
    *   6– < 10 Years : 240 Days of Wages
    *   10+ years → 300 days’ wages.
*   **Exemptions:** No severance if employee resigns voluntarily, is terminated for serious misconduct, or is on probation (< 120 Days)
*   **Special Cases:** Mass layoffs require prior notice and sometimes government approval**.**

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   **Resignation:** Must give notice equal to one pay cycle.
*   **Compliance:** Employees must follow company work rules and safety regulations.
*   **Misconduct:** Theft, fraud, violence, or gross negligence can forfeit severance entitlement.
*   **BPJS Equivalent:** Employees must contribute to Thai Social Security Fund.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   **Labor Inspections:** Ministry of Labour conducts audits.
*   **Penalties:** Fines and imprisonment for non‑compliance.
*   **Dispute Resolution:** Labour Court system; mediation encouraged first.
*   **Rare Note:** Employees can claim unfair dismissal if termination lacks valid grounds.

**Thailand – Additional Clauses & Details**

**1\. Annual Leave**

*   **Article 30 LPA**: Employees with ≥1 year service → minimum 6 working days annual leave.
*   → leave is discretionary, but employers must grant “suitable” leave.
*   Employers may increase leave based on tenure or company policy.

**2\. Sick Leave**

*   **Article 32 LPA**: Employees entitled to 30 days paid sick leave per year.
*   If sick leave exceeds 3 days, employer may request a medical certificate.
*   Sick leave does not reduce annual leave entitlement.

**3\. Maternity & Paternity**

*   **Article 41 LPA**: Maternity leave 98 days (including holidays). Employer pays 45 days, remainder covered by Social Security Fund.
*   **2025 amendment**: Paternity leave introduced → 15 days paid.

**4\. Overtime & Holiday Work**

*   **Article 61 LPA**: Overtime pay multipliers:
    *   1.5× hourly wage → overtime on normal working days.
    *   2× hourly wage → work on holidays during normal hours.
    *   3× hourly wage → work on holidays beyond normal hours.
*   **Article 62 LPA**: Employees must consent to overtime, except in urgent cases.

**5\. Severance Pay**

*   **Article 118 LPA**: Mandatory severance after 120 days continuous service.
*   **Exemptions**: No severance if employee resigns voluntarily, is terminated for serious misconduct, or is on probation.

**6**. **Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company work rules (Article 108 LPA requires employers to publish rules if ≥10 employees).
*   Must give notice equal to one pay cycle for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence, gross negligence) can forfeit severance entitlement.

**7\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Employers must maintain records of working hours, wages, and leave (Article 23 LPA).
*   Labour inspectors have authority to audit and impose fines.
*   Disputes go to Labour Court; unfair dismissal claims are possible if termination lacks valid grounds.

**Bottom Line**

The information is solid for HR operations, but for **full compliance** you should also track:

*   **Articles 30, 32, 41, 61, 62, 108, 118** of the LPA.
*   Ministerial regulations (especially on overtime calculation, severance, and leave).
*   Royal Gazette updates (for amendments and wage changes).
