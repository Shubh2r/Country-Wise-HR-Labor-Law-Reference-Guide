**Country Name: New Zealand**

**🇳🇿 New Zealand – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Employment Relations Act 2000_ – core employment law.
    *   _Holidays Act 2003_ – leave and holiday entitlements.
    *   _Minimum Wage Act 1983_.
    *   _Health and Safety at Work Act 2015_.
    *   _KiwiSaver Act 2006_ – retirement savings.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment (MBIE).
*   Applicability: Covers all employees, including part‑time, fixed‑term, and casual.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (full‑time, part‑time).
    *   Fixed‑term (must have genuine reason and specified end date).
    *   Casual (no guaranteed hours).
*   Probationary Periods: Common practice 3 months; must be specified in contract.
*   Trial Periods:
    *   Up to 90 days allowed for employers with ≤19 employees.
    *   Must be agreed in writing at the start of employment.
*   Mandatory Clauses: job description, hours, wages, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: English; bilingual contracts allowed.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: No statutory maximum; “reasonable hours” must be agreed in contract.
*   Overtime Rules: No statutory overtime rate; must be contractually agreed.
*   Rest Breaks (ERA 2000):
*   10‑minute paid rest break for every 4 hours worked.
*   30‑minute unpaid meal break for shifts over 6 hours.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave (Holidays Act 2003):
    *   4 weeks/year after 12 months continuous service.
*   Public Holidays: 12 annually (e.g., Waitangi Day, ANZAC Day).
*   Sick Leave:
    *   10 days/year after 6 months continuous service.
    *   Can carry over up to 20 days.
*   Bereavement Leave:
    *   3 days for close family.
    *   1 day for others.
*   Parental Leave:
*   Up to 26 weeks government‑paid parental leave.
*   Extended leave up to 52 weeks (job protected).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Set annually; ~NZD 23/hour (2025).
*   Overtime Pay: Only if contractually agreed.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   KiwiSaver (retirement savings, 3% minimum employer contribution).
    *   ACC (Accident Compensation Corporation) levies for workplace injuries.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common practice in some industries.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Period: Typically 2–4 weeks; must be specified in contract.
*   Redundancy Pay: Not mandated by law; depends on contract or collective agreement.
*   Trial Periods: Employers can dismiss within 90 days without personal grievance claims (small businesses only).
*   Exemptions: No redundancy pay if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal.
*   Employees must contribute to KiwiSaver unless they opt out.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectors (MBIE): Monitor compliance with ERA and Holidays Act.
*   Penalties: Fines for breaches (e.g., unpaid wages, leave violations).
*   Dispute Resolution: Mediation → Employment Relations Authority → Employment Court.

**🇳🇿 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Employment Relations Act 2000**:
    *   Requires “good faith” in all employment relationships.
    *   Governs collective bargaining and union rights.
*   **Holidays Act 2003**:
    *   Complex rules on holiday pay calculations (average vs ordinary weekly pay).
    *   Employers often face audits for miscalculated holiday pay.
*   **KiwiSaver Act 2006**:
    *   Employees auto‑enrolled unless they opt out.
    *   Employer minimum contribution 3%, but many contribute more.
*   **ACC (Accident Compensation Corporation)**:
    *   Provides no‑fault workplace injury compensation.
    *   Funded by employer levies.
*   **Recent Developments**:
*   Minimum wage adjusted annually (NZD 23/hour in 2025).
*   Sick leave entitlement increased from 5 to 10 days (2021 reform).
*   Ongoing government reviews of Holidays Act to simplify pay calculations.
