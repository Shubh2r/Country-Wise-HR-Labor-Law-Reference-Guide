**Country Name: India**

**🇮🇳 India – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

**•** Primary Laws (post‑2020 reforms):

• Code on Wages, 2019 (minimum wage, equal remuneration, payment of wages).

• Industrial Relations Code, 2020 (trade unions, standing orders, dispute resolution).

• Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code, 2020.

• Social Security Code, 2020 (PF, ESI, gratuity, maternity benefits).

• Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour & Employment (MoLE).

• Applicability: Codes consolidate 29 central labor laws; states issue rules for implementation.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

**•** Types of Employment:

• Permanent.

• Fixed‑term (explicitly recognized under IR Code).

• Contractual/outsourced.

• Apprenticeship (Apprentices Act, 1961).

• Probationary Periods: Typically 6 months; can extend to 1 year depending on standing orders, but must be specified in appointment letter.

• Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, benefits, termination conditions.

• Language Requirement: English or local language; bilingual contracts common.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

**•** Standard Hours: 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week (OSH Code).

• Overtime Rules:

• 2× ordinary rate → overtime beyond prescribed hours.

• Weekly Rest: 1 day off per week.

• Exemptions: Managerial staff may be excluded.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

**•** Annual Leave (Factories Act/OSH Code):

• 1 day for every 20 days worked (after 240 days service in a year).

• Casual Leave: Typically 7–10 days (policy‑driven, not statutory).

• Sick Leave: 12 days/year under Shops & Establishments Acts (varies by state).

• Public Holidays: 10–15 annually (national + state).

• Maternity Leave (Maternity Benefit Act, now under Social Security Code): 26 weeks paid leave; 12 weeks for 3rd child onwards.

• Paternity Leave: Not yet codified nationally; some organizations provide 15 days.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

**•** Minimum Wage: Set by central and state governments under Code on Wages.

• Overtime Pay: 2× ordinary rate.

• Social Security Contributions:

• Provident Fund (EPF).

• Employees’ State Insurance (ESI).

• Gratuity (mandatory after 5 years service).

• Maternity benefits.

• Bonus: Payment of Bonus Act → 8.33%–20% of wages for eligible employees.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

**•** Notice Period:

• 1 month for permanent employees (varies by standing orders).

• Fixed‑term contracts end automatically on expiry.

• Severance/Gratuity:

• Gratuity payable after 5 years continuous service → 15 days wages per year of service.

• Retrenchment Compensation (IR Code):

• 15 days average pay per year of service.

• Exemptions: No gratuity if <5 years service (except death/disability).

**7\. Employee Obligations**

**•** Must comply with standing orders and company rules.

• Must give notice for resignation (usually 30 days).

• Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.

• Employees must contribute to PF and ESI if covered.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

**•** Labour Inspections: Conducted by MoLE and state labor departments.

• Penalties: Fines or imprisonment for violations.

• Dispute Resolution: Labour Courts, Industrial Tribunals.

• Recent Reform Note: Codes aim to simplify compliance via online portals and unified registers.

**🇮🇳 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Standing Orders (Industrial Relations Code, 2020)**:
    *   Define categories of workers (permanent, probationary, fixed‑term, apprentice, temporary).
    *   Specify misconduct grounds and disciplinary procedures.
*   **Gratuity (Social Security Code, 2020)**:
    *   Payable after 5 years continuous service.
    *   Exceptions: payable earlier in case of death or disability.
*   **Retrenchment Compensation (IR Code)**:
    *   15 days average pay per year of service for layoffs/redundancy.
*   **State Variations**:
    *   Shops & Establishments Acts differ by state (leave entitlements, working hours, holidays).
    *   Example: Maharashtra allows 21 days earned leave after 240 days service.
*   **Equal Remuneration (Code on Wages)**:
    *   Prohibits gender‑based wage discrimination.
*   **Occupational Safety (OSH Code)**:
    *   Detailed provisions on workplace safety, welfare facilities, and working conditions.
*   **Recent Developments**:
*   Labour Codes (2019–2020) are in phased implementation; some states have notified rules, others are pending.
*   Digital compliance portals being rolled out for unified filings.

*   Gratuity and retrenchment rules under Social Security & IR Codes.
