**🇰🇷 South Korea – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Law: Labor Standards Act (LSA), with Enforcement Decrees.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Employment and Labor (MOEL).
*   Applicability Thresholds:
*   LSA applies to workplaces with ≥5 employees.
*   Workplaces with < 5 Employees are exempt from many provisions (Annual Leave, Severance, Overtime Rules)

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Contracts:
    *   Regular (indefinite) – standard employment.
    *   Fixed‑term – max 2 years; beyond that, employee becomes regular.
    *   Dispatched/Agency – regulated under the Act on the Protection of Dispatched Workers.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, working hours, holidays, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Korean preferred; bilingual contracts common.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week, 8 hours/day.
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   Max 12 hours/week overtime.
    *   Multiplier:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime on normal working days.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → work on holidays or rest days.
*   Rest Periods: At least 1 hour break for every 4 hours worked.
*   Exemptions: Certain managerial/executive roles may be excluded.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave (Article 60 LSA):
    *   <1 Year Service → 1 Day Leave Per Month Worked.
    *   ≥1 year service → 15 days paid annual leave.
    *   After 3 years continuous service → +1 day per 2 years, capped at 25 days.
*   Public Holidays: 15 recognized national holidays; substitute day provisions apply.
*   Sick Leave: Not mandated by statute; often provided by company policy.
*   Maternity Leave: 90 days (45 before, 45 after childbirth); extended to 120 days for multiple or premature births. Employer pays 60 days, social insurance covers remainder.
*   Paternity Leave: 20 days (doubled in 2026).
*   Family Care Leave: Expanded in 2026 to cover subfertility treatment and broader family care.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Updated annually by MOEL.
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Bonuses: Not mandatory, but common.
*   Social Insurance Contributions:
    *   National Pension.
    *   National Health Insurance.
    *   Employment Insurance.
    *   Industrial Accident Compensation Insurance.
*   Rare Note: Supreme Court ruled in 2026 that performance‑based pay does not count toward “ordinary wages” for severance calculation.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Period: At least 30 days.
*   Grounds for Termination: Must be “just cause” (economic necessity, misconduct, redundancy).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   Mandatory for employees with ≥1 year continuous service.
    *   Formula: Average wages × years of service (at least 30 days’ wages per year).

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company work rules (required for workplaces with ≥10 employees).
*   Must give notice for resignation (usually 30 days).
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must participate in social insurance programs.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labor Inspections: Conducted by MOEL.
*   Penalties: Fines or imprisonment for violations.
*   Dispute Resolution: Labor Relations Commission → courts.
*   Rare Note: Employees can file anonymous complaints with MOEL.

3\. Overtime & Holiday Work

*   **Overtime cap**: 12 hours/week.
*   **Multiplier**:
    *   _1.5×_ → overtime on normal working days.
    *   _2×_ → work on holidays or rest days.
*   **Consent**: Employee consent required for overtime, except in emergencies.
*   **Rare Note**: Certain industries (transport, healthcare) have special overtime rules.

4\. Severance Pay (Retirement Benefit)

*   **Mandatory if**:
    *   Employee has ≥1 year continuous service.
    *   Workplace has ≥5 employees.
*   **Formula**: Average wages × years of service (minimum 30 days’ wages per year).
*   **Exemptions**:  
    <1 Year of Service – No Severance Pay Required

<5 Employee’s – No Severance Mandatory

*   **Recent Supreme Court ruling (2026)**: Performance‑based pay does not count toward “ordinary wages” for severance calculation.

5\. Employee Obligations

*   **Resignation**: Must give reasonable notice (usually 30 days).
*   **Compliance**: Must follow company work rules (mandatory for ≥10 employees).
*   **Misconduct**: Fraud, theft, violence, or gross negligence → dismissal without severance.
*   **Social Insurance**: Employees must participate in National Pension, Health Insurance, Employment Insurance, Industrial Accident Insurance.

6\. Leave Beyond Annual

*   **Maternity Leave**: 90 days (extended to 120 for multiple/premature births). Employer pays 60 days, social insurance covers remainder.
*   **Paternity Leave**: 20 days (expanded in 2026).
*   **Family Care Leave**: Expanded in 2026 to cover subfertility treatment and broader family care.
*   **Childcare Leave**: Up to 1 year unpaid, with partial wage replacement via Employment Insurance.

7\. Compliance & Enforcement

*   **Labor Inspections**: MOEL audits contracts, payroll, leave records.
*   **Penalties**: Fines or imprisonment for violations.
*   **Dispute Resolution**: Labor Relations Commission → courts.
*   **Rare Note**: Employees can file anonymous complaints with MOEL.
