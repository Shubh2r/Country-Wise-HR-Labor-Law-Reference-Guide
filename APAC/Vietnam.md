**Country Name: Vietnam**

**🇻🇳 Vietnam – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Code 2019_ (effective 2021, current framework).
    *   _Social Insurance Law 2014_.
    *   _Trade Union Law 2012_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour, Invalids and Social Affairs (MOLISA).
*   Applicability: Covers all employees, both domestic and foreign, working in Vietnam.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   **Types of Contracts:**
    *   Indefinite term.
    *   Fixed‑term (12–36 months).
    *   Seasonal or specific‑job contracts (<12 month’s)
*   **Probationary Periods:**
    *   Up to 180 days for managerial positions.
    *   30–60 days for skilled jobs.
    *   6 days for unskilled jobs.
*   **Mandatory Clauses:** job description, workplace, wages, working hours, social insurance, termination conditions.
*   **Language Requirement:** Must be in Vietnamese; bilingual contracts allowed but Vietnamese version prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week.
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime on normal working days.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → overtime on weekly rest days.
    *   _3×_ hourly wage → overtime on public holidays.
*   Overtime Cap: 40 hours/month, 200 hours/year (300 hours/year in special sectors).

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave (Labour Code Article 113):
    *   12 days/year (for employees working in normal conditions).
    *   14 days/year (for employees under hazardous conditions).
    *   16 days/year (for employees under extremely hazardous conditions).
    *   +1 day for every 5 years of service.
*   Public Holidays: 11 days annually (Tet, National Day, etc.).
*   Sick Leave: Covered by social insurance; entitlement depends on contribution period and workplace conditions.
*   Maternity Leave: 6 months (extended for multiple births).
*   Paternity Leave: 5–14 days depending on circumstances.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Region‑based, set annually by government.
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social Insurance.
    *   Health Insurance.
    *   Unemployment Insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but widely practiced.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Period:
    *   45 days for indefinite contracts.
    *   30 days for fixed‑term contracts.
    *   3 days for seasonal contracts.
*   Severance Pay:
    *   ½ month’s wages per year of service (if employed ≥12 months).
*   Job Loss Allowance:
    *   1 month’s wages per year of service (minimum 2 months) if redundancy/closure.
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give notice for resignation (same as employer notice periods).
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to social insurance, health insurance, and unemployment insurance.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate (MOLISA): Monitors compliance.
*   Penalties: Fines or suspension of operations for violations.
*   Dispute Resolution: Labour mediator → Labour Arbitration Council → courts.

**9\. References**

*   Labour Code 2019 (Vietnam) – MOLISA.
*   Social Insurance Law 2014 – National Assembly of Vietnam.
*   MOLISA Official Site: [http://www.molisa.gov.vn](https://www.molisa.gov.vn/)

**🇻🇳 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Labour Code 2019**:
    *   Article 20: Limits on fixed‑term contract renewals (after 2 renewals, must convert to indefinite).
    *   Article 35: Employee resignation rights with proper notice.
    *   Article 36: Employer termination grounds (redundancy, misconduct, incapacity).
*   **Social Insurance Law 2014**:
    *   Employer contributions: 17.5% of salary.
    *   Employee contributions: 8% of salary.
*   **Trade Union Law 2012**:
    *   Employees have the right to form or join trade unions.
*   **Foreign Employees**:
    *   Work permits required (valid up to 2 years, renewable).
    *   Employers must justify hiring foreigners if no suitable local candidates.
*   **Recent Developments**:
*   Minimum wage adjusted annually by region (Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City highest).
*   Expanded paternity leave (up to 14 days for multiple births).
*   Stronger enforcement of overtime caps under Labour Code 2019.
