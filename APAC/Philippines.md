**Country Name: Philippines**

**🇵🇭 Philippines – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Law: Labor Code of the Philippines (Presidential Decree No. 442, as amended).
*   Regulatory Authority: Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE).
*   Applicability: Applies to all employers, with some exemptions for small family businesses and domestic workers (covered separately under the _Domestic Workers Act_).

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Contracts:
    *   Regular employment – default status after 6 months probation unless terminated for just cause.
    *   Probationary employment – max 6 months; must specify standards for regularization.
    *   Fixed‑term/project employment – allowed if duration/project is clearly defined.
    *   Casual employment – for work not usually necessary to the business; becomes regular if >1 year.
*   Mandatory Clauses: job description, wages, working hours, benefits, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: English or Filipino; bilingual contracts common.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week.
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.25×_ hourly wage → overtime on regular working days.
    *   _1.3×_ hourly wage → overtime on rest days or special holidays.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → work on regular holidays.
    *   _2.6×_ hourly wage → overtime on regular holidays.
*   Night Shift Differential: +10% for work between 10 PM–6 AM.
*   Exemptions: Managerial employees and field personnel may be excluded.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Service Incentive Leave (SIL):
    *   5 days paid leave annually after 1 year of service.
    *   Convertible to cash if unused.
*   Expanded Maternity Leave Law (RA 11210): 105 days with pay; additional 15 days for solo mothers.
*   Paternity Leave Law (RA 8187): 7 days with pay for first 4 deliveries.
*   Parental Leave for Solo Parents (RA 8972): 7 days annually.
*   Special Leave for Women (RA 9710): 2 months for gynecological disorders.
*   Public Holidays: 18–20 annually (regular + special non‑working days).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Region‑based, set by Regional Tripartite Wages and Productivity Boards.
*   13th Month Pay: Mandatory for all rank‑and‑file employees, payable by December 24.
*   Social Security Contributions:
    *   SSS (Social Security System).
    *   PhilHealth (health insurance).
    *   Pag‑IBIG Fund (housing savings).
*   Overtime & Holiday Pay: As above.

**6\. Termination & Separation/Severance**

*   Security of Tenure: Employees cannot be dismissed except for just or authorized cause, with due process.
*   Separation Pay (mandatory in certain cases):
    *   Authorized causes (redundancy, retrenchment, closure, disease):
        *   Redundancy/closure → 1 month pay or 1 month per year of service (whichever higher).
        *   Retrenchment/disease → ½ month pay per year of service.
    *   Just causes (misconduct, neglect, fraud, crime) → no separation pay.
*   Severance Pay: Not mandated by law; discretionary unless provided by company policy or CBA.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and regulations.
*   Must give reasonable notice for resignation (usually 30 days).
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without separation pay.
*   Employees must contribute to SSS, PhilHealth, and Pag‑IBIG.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   DOLE inspections: Check contracts, payroll, benefits, and leave records.
*   Penalties: Fines, closure orders, or criminal liability for violations.
*   Dispute Resolution: Labor Arbiter → National Labor Relations Commission (NLRC) → courts.
*   Rare Note: Employees can file anonymous complaints with DOLE.

**🇵🇭 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know:**

*   **Security of Tenure (Article 279)**: Employees cannot be dismissed except for just or authorized cause, with due process.
*   **Due Process in Termination**: Requires _notice to explain_ + _opportunity to be heard_ + _notice of termination_.
*   **Holiday Pay Rules (Articles 94–96)**: Distinguish between regular holidays (100% pay even if not worked) and special non‑working days (no pay unless worked).
*   **Service Incentive Leave (Article 95)**: Applies to employees with ≥1 year service, except managerial staff, field personnel, and those already enjoying equivalent leave.
*   **Separation Pay (Articles 283–284)**: Detailed formulas for redundancy, retrenchment, closure, and disease.
*   **Probationary Employment (Article 296)**: Standards for regularization must be communicated at hiring; otherwise, employee becomes regular.
*   **Contractualization Rules (DOLE Department Orders)**: Regulate project and fixed‑term employment to prevent abuse.
*   **Special Laws**:
*   _RA 10911_ – Anti‑Age Discrimination in Employment.
*   _RA 11058_ – Occupational Safety and Health Standards Law.
*   _RA 11165_ – Telecommuting Act (work‑from‑home rights).
