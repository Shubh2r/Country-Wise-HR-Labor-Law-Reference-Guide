**Country Name: Myanmar**

**🇲🇲 Myanmar – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Factories Act, 1951_ – workplace safety.
    *   _Shops and Establishments Act, 1951_ – working hours, leave.
    *   _Employment and Skills Development Law, 2013_.
    *   _Minimum Wage Law, 2013_.
    *   _Social Security Law, 2012_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour, Immigration and Population.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.
*   Recent Developments (2025–26):
*   Minimum wage adjustments under review.
*   Expansion of social security coverage.
*   Strengthening of occupational safety standards.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment: Permanent, fixed‑term, casual/seasonal.
*   Probationary Periods: Typically 3 months.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Burmese mandatory.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 8 hours/day, 44–48 hours/week depending on sector.
*   Overtime Rules: _2×_ hourly wage.
*   Rest Periods: Weekly rest day (usually Sunday).

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave: 10 days/year (after 12 months of service).
*   Public Holidays: ~15 annually (Independence Day, Thingyan Water Festival, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: 30 days/year (paid via social security).
*   Maternity Leave: 14 weeks (paid).
*   Paternity Leave: 15 days (paid).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: ~MMK 4,800/day (2023), under review for 2026.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social Security Fund: employer ~3–5%, employee ~2%.
    *   Covers health, pension, accident, maternity.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in practice.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods: ~1 month for permanent employees.
*   Severance Pay: 30 days’ wages per year of service (redundancy/unfair dismissal).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation (~1 month).
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate: Oversees compliance.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding in unionised workplaces.
