**Country Name: Singapore**

**🇸🇬 Singapore – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Employment Act (Cap. 91)_ – core employment law.
    *   _Work Injury Compensation Act (WICA)_.
    *   _Industrial Relations Act_.
    *   _Central Provident Fund (CPF) Act_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Manpower (MOM).
*   Applicability: Employment Act covers employees earning ≤SGD 4,500/month (non‑managerial), but many provisions apply more broadly.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent.
    *   Fixed‑term.
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Contract/temporary.
*   Probationary Periods: Not legally capped; common practice 3–6 months.
*   Mandatory Clauses: job scope, wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: English is standard; bilingual contracts allowed.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours (Employment Act): 8 hours/day, 44 hours/week.
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly basic rate → overtime beyond 44 hours/week.
    *   Max 72 overtime hours/month.
*   Rest Days: At least 1 day off per week.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave (Employment Act):
    *   1 year service → 7 days.
    *   Increases by 1 day per year up to 14 days after 8 years.
*   Public Holidays: 11 gazetted days annually.
*   Sick Leave:
    *   14 days outpatient/year.
    *   60 days hospitalization/year.
*   Maternity Leave: 16 weeks (paid, if employed ≥3 months).
*   Paternity Leave: 2 weeks (paid, if employed ≥3 months).
*   Shared Parental Leave: Up to 4 weeks transferable from mother to father.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: No national minimum wage; Progressive Wage Model applies to certain sectors (cleaning, security, retail, etc.).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Central Provident Fund (CPF) – employer and employee contributions.
    *   Skills Development Levy (SDL).
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common practice.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Period (Employment Act):
    *   <26 Weeks Service → 1 Day
    *   26 weeks–2 years → 1 week.
    *   2–5 years → 2 weeks.
    *   ≥5 years → 4 weeks.
*   Severance Pay: Not mandated by law; retrenchment benefits depend on contract or collective agreement.
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give notice for resignation (same as employer notice periods).
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to CPF unless exempt (e.g., foreigners on work passes).

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   MOM inspections: Contracts, wages, leave records.
*   Penalties: Fines or imprisonment for violations (e.g., unpaid wages, CPF non‑compliance).
*   Dispute Resolution: Tripartite Alliance for Dispute Management (TADM) → Employment Claims Tribunal.

**9\. References**

*   Ministry of Manpower (MOM): [https://www.mom.gov.sg](https://www.mom.gov.sg/)

*   Central Provident Fund (CPF): [https://www.cpf.gov.sg](https://www.cpf.gov.sg/)

**🇸🇬 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Employment Act coverage**:
    *   Applies mainly to employees earning ≤SGD 4,500/month (non‑managerial).
    *   Managers and executives may not be covered by overtime provisions but are still protected for basic rights (salary payment, leave, etc.).
*   **Retrenchment Benefits**:
    *   Not statutory, but MOM guidelines recommend 2 weeks–1 month’s salary per year of service.
*   **Childcare Leave**:
    *   6 days/year for parents of children under 7 (if employed ≥3 months).
    *   Extended childcare leave: 2 days/year for children aged 7–12.
*   **Adoption Leave**:
    *   12 weeks for mothers adopting a child under 12 months.
*   **Foreign Employees**:
    *   Must hold valid work passes (Employment Pass, S Pass, Work Permit).
    *   Employers must meet quota and levy requirements for Work Permit and S Pass holders.
*   **Recent Developments**:
*   Progressive Wage Model expanded to retail, food services, and waste management sectors.
*   CPF contribution rates gradually increasing to enhance retirement adequacy.
*   Enhanced retrenchment reporting requirements introduced in 2024.
