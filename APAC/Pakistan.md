**Country Name: Pakistan**

**🇵🇰 Pakistan – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Factories Act, 1934_ (hours, safety, welfare).
    *   _Shops and Establishments Ordinances_ (provincial).
    *   _Industrial and Commercial Employment (Standing Orders) Ordinance, 1968_.
    *   _Payment of Wages Act, 1936_.
    *   _Employees’ Old‑Age Benefits Institution (EOBI) Act, 1976_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Overseas Pakistanis & Human Resource Development, plus provincial labor departments.
*   Applicability: Labor law is partly federal, partly provincial (after 18th Amendment, 2010).

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Contracts:
    *   Permanent – after probation (usually 3–6 months).
    *   Probationary – max 6 months.
    *   Fixed‑term/project – allowed if duration/project is specified.
    *   Apprenticeship – regulated under Apprenticeship Ordinance, 1962.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, benefits, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Urdu or English; bilingual contracts common.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week (Factories Act).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → overtime beyond 9 hours/day or 48 hours/week.
*   Weekly Rest: 1 day off per week (usually Sunday).
*   Exemptions: Managerial staff may be excluded.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   14 days paid annual leave after 12 months continuous service.
*   Casual Leave: 10 days/year.
*   Sick Leave: 16 days/year with full pay.
*   Public Holidays: 14–16 annually (Eid, Independence Day, etc.).
*   Maternity Leave: 12 weeks (6 before, 6 after childbirth).
*   Paternity Leave: Introduced in 2021 → 10 days.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Set by provincial governments; ~PKR 32,000/month (2025).
*   Overtime Pay: 2× hourly wage.
*   Social Security Contributions:
    *   EOBI (pension).
    *   Provincial Social Security Institutions (medical, injury).
*   Gratuity: 1 month’s wages per year of service (if no provident fund).
*   Provident Fund: Employer and employee contributions (if scheme exists).

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Period: 1 month (or wages in lieu).
*   Severance/Gratuity:
    *   30 days’ wages per year of service (if no provident fund).
*   Authorized Causes: redundancy, closure, retrenchment.
*   Just Causes: misconduct, fraud, habitual absence → no severance.
*   Exemptions: Daily wage workers may not qualify for severance.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with standing orders and company rules.
*   Must give 1 month’s notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to EOBI and provincial social security if covered.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labor Inspections: Provincial labor departments enforce compliance.
*   Penalties: Fines or imprisonment for violations.
*   Dispute Resolution: Labor Courts → High Courts.
*   Rare Note: Collective bargaining and trade unions protected under Industrial Relations Acts.

**🇵🇰 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Standing Orders Ordinance, 1968**: Defines categories of workers (permanent, probationary, temporary, apprentice, badli/relief workers) and their rights.
*   **Factories Act, 1934**: Detailed provisions on health, safety, welfare, working hours, and child labor restrictions.
*   **Shops & Establishments Ordinances (provincial)**: Regulate working hours, leave, and holidays for non‑factory establishments (e.g., offices, retail).
*   **Payment of Wages Act, 1936**: Rules on timely wage payment, permissible deductions.
*   **Industrial Relations Acts (provincial)**: Collective bargaining, trade union rights, dispute resolution.
*   **EOBI Act, 1976**: Employer contributions (5% of minimum wage) and employee pension entitlements.
*   **Social Security Ordinances (provincial)**: Medical care, injury compensation, maternity benefits.
*   **Recent Developments**:
*   Minimum wage increases are set annually by provincial governments.
*   Paternity leave (10 days) introduced in 2021, but implementation varies by province.
*   Occupational safety and health reforms are being piloted in Punjab and Sindh.
