**Country Name: Poland**

**🇵🇱 Poland – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Code (Kodeks pracy)_ – core employment framework.
    *   _Act on Social Security System_.
    *   _Act on Trade Unions_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Family, Labour and Social Policy.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (max 33 months, up to 3 contracts).
    *   Probationary (max 3 months).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/agency work.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Polish is mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed but Polish prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (8 hours/day).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime weekdays.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → overtime Sundays/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 35 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   20 days/year (<10 years service)
    *   26 days/year (≥10 years service).
*   Public Holidays: 13 annually (e.g., Constitution Day, Independence Day, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: Paid; employer covers 33 days/year, then social security.
*   Maternity Leave: 20 weeks (paid).
*   Paternity Leave: 2 weeks (paid).
*   Parental Leave: Up to 41 weeks combined (job protected, partially paid).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted annually (~PLN 4,300/month gross in 2025).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (employer ~20%, employee ~13%).
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in public sector and CBAs.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   2 weeks (<6 months service)
    *   1 month (6 months–3 years).
    *   3 months (≥3 years).
*   Severance Pay (redundancy):
    *   1 month’s wages (< 2 years of service)
    *   2 months (2–8 years).
    *   3 months (≥8 years).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate (Państwowa Inspekcja Pracy): Enforces compliance with labour law.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.
