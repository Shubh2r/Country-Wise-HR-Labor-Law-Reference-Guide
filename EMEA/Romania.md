**Country Name: Romania**

**🇷🇴 Romania – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Code (Codul Muncii)_ – core employment framework.
    *   _Social Security Law_.
    *   _Collective Bargaining Agreements (CBAs)_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour and Social Solidarity.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (max 36 months).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/agency work.
*   Probationary Periods:
    *   90 days (regular employees).
    *   120 days (management roles).
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Romanian is mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed but Romanian prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (8 hours/day).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.75×_ hourly wage → overtime weekdays.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → weekends/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 12 hours daily rest; 48 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   20 working days/year minimum.
*   Public Holidays: 15 annually (e.g., National Day, Easter, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: Paid; employer covers first 5 days, then social security.
*   Maternity Leave: 126 days (paid at 85% of average income).
*   Paternity Leave: 10 days (paid).
*   Parental Leave: Up to 2 years (paid at 85% of average income, capped).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted annually (~RON 3,700/month gross in 2025).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (employer ~2.25%, employee ~35%).
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common under CBAs.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   20 working days (employees).
    *   45 working days (management).
*   Severance Pay: Not statutory; redundancy compensation often negotiated via CBAs.
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate (Inspecția Muncii): Enforces compliance with labour law.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.

*   **Social Security Law**: Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   **Collective Bargaining Agreements (CBAs)**: Define sector‑specific wages, bonuses, and entitlements.
*   **Language Requirement**: Contracts must be in Romanian; bilingual versions allowed but Romanian prevails.
