**Country Name: Denmark**

**🇩🇰 Denmark – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   **Primary Laws:**
    *   _Danish Salaried Employees Act (Funktionærloven)_ – contracts, termination, severance.
    *   _Working Environment Act_ – health & safety, working conditions.
    *   _Holiday Act (Ferieloven)_ – annual leave.
    *   _Act on Posting of Workers_ – rules for foreign service providers.
*   **Regulatory Authority:** Ministry of Employment; Danish Working Environment Authority.
*   **Applicability:** Covers employees in Denmark; CBAs are highly influential and often override statutory minimums.
*   **Recent Developments (2025–26):**
*   Amendments to the _Working Environment Act_ (accident reporting delayed until 3rd day of absence).
*   Stricter requirements for posted workers (mandatory RUT registration before work begins).
*   Reforms to _Labour Market Supplementary Pension (ATP)_ and Workers’ Compensation Insurance for transparency and cybersecurity.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (allowed if justified).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/seasonal work.
*   Probationary Periods: Typically 3 months (can be shorter or longer via CBAs).
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Danish is standard; bilingual contracts allowed.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 37 hours/week (common under CBAs).
*   Overtime Rules: No statutory premium; rates set by CBAs or contracts.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 24 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   5 weeks/year minimum (25 days).
*   Public Holidays: 10–12 annually (e.g., Constitution Day, Christmas, Easter).
*   Sick Leave: Paid by employer for first 30 days; then municipal benefits.
*   Maternity Leave: 4 weeks before and 14 weeks after birth (paid).
*   Paternity Leave: 2 weeks (paid).
*   Parental Leave: Up to 32 weeks shared, with benefits.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: No statutory minimum; wages set by CBAs.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Labour Market Supplementary Pension (ATP).
    *   Social security contributions (employer and employee).
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common under CBAs.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods (Salaried Employees Act):
    *   1 month (<6 months service)
    *   3 months (6 months–3 years).
    *   4 months (3–6 years).
    *   5 months (6–9 years).
    *   6 months (≥9 years).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   1–3 months’ salary depending on tenure (10–17 years).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Employees must contribute to ATP and social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Danish Working Environment Authority: Enforces health & safety.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.

**🇩🇰 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Salaried Employees Act (Funktionærloven)**: Governs contracts, notice, severance.
*   **Holiday Act (Ferieloven)**: Guarantees 5 weeks of annual leave.
*   **Working Environment Act**: Workplace safety and accident reporting.
*   **Language Requirement**: Contracts typically in Danish; bilingual versions allowed.
*   **Recent Developments (2025–26)**:
*   Accident reporting under the Working Environment Act now required only after 3 days’ absence.
*   Posted workers must register in the RUT system before starting work.
*   ATP and Workers’ Compensation Insurance reforms for transparency and cybersecurity.
