**Country Name: Switzerland**

**🇨🇭 Switzerland – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Swiss Code of Obligations (CO)_ – contracts, termination, severance.
    *   _Labour Act (ArG)_ – working hours, rest, health & safety.
    *   _Federal Act on Accident Insurance (UVG)_.
*   Regulatory Authority: State Secretariat for Economic Affairs (SECO).
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs are common and influential.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (valid until agreed end date).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/seasonal work.
*   Probationary Periods: Max 3 months.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Contracts typically in German, French, or Italian depending on canton; bilingual versions allowed.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours:
    *   45 hours/week (industrial, office, technical staff).
    *   50 hours/week (other employees).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   Paid at _1.25×_ hourly wage or compensated with time off.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 35 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   4 weeks/year minimum.
    *   5 weeks/year (<20years old)
*   Public Holidays: Vary by canton (8–15 days).
*   Sick Leave: Paid via employer (duration depends on tenure) and insurance.
*   Maternity Leave: 14 weeks (paid at 80% via social insurance).
*   Paternity Leave: 2 weeks (paid at 80%).
*   Parental Leave: No statutory parental leave beyond maternity/paternity, but CBAs may provide extensions.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: No federal minimum; some cantons (e.g., Geneva, Neuchâtel) set minimums (~CHF 23/hour in 2025).
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (AHV/IV/EO, employer ~5.3%, employee ~5.3%).
    *   Accident insurance (UVG).
    *   Pension fund contributions (BVG).
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in practice.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods (CO):
    *   1 month (<1 year service)
    *   2 months (1–9 years).
    *   3 months (≥10 years).
*   Severance Pay: Not statutory; CBAs or contracts may provide redundancy compensation.
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Employees must contribute to social security and pension funds.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   SECO: Oversees labour law compliance.
*   Cantonal Labour Inspectorates: Enforce working time and safety rules.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.

**🇨🇭 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Swiss Code of Obligations (CO)**: Governs contracts, notice, termination.
*   **Labour Act (ArG)**: Sets limits on hours, rest, and workplace safety.
*   **Federal Act on Accident Insurance (UVG)**: Covers occupational injuries.
*   **Language Requirement**: Contracts typically in German, French, or Italian depending on canton.
*   **Recent Developments**:
*   Cantonal minimum wage increases (especially Geneva, Neuchâtel, Jura, Ticino).
*   Pension reforms — gradual increase in retirement age for women to 65 (aligned with men).
*   Stronger EU alignment in workplace safety and equality directives.
