**Country Name: Austria**

**🇦🇹 Austria – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Employment Contract Law (Angestelltengesetz)_.
    *   _Working Time Act (Arbeitszeitgesetz)_.
    *   _Holidays Act (Urlaubsgesetz)_.
    *   _Labour Constitution Act (Arbeitsverfassungsgesetz)_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Federal Ministry of Labour & Economy.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; collective bargaining agreements (CBAs) play a major role.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (full‑time, part‑time).
    *   Fixed‑term (must have objective justification).
    *   Temporary.
*   Probationary Periods: Typically 1–3 months; must be specified in contract.
*   Mandatory Clauses: job description, wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: German is standard; bilingual contracts allowed but German version prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (8 hours/day).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → work on Sundays/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 36 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave (Urlaubsgesetz):
    *   25 working days/year (after 6 months service).
    *   Increases to 30 days after 25 years of service.
*   Public Holidays: 13 annually (e.g., New Year’s Day, National Day, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: Paid; employer covers initial period, then social insurance.
*   Maternity Leave: 16 weeks (8 before, 8 after birth).
*   Parental Leave: Up to 2 years (job protected).
*   Paternity Leave: 1 month (Familienzeitbonus).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: No statutory minimum; set by CBAs (generally ≥€1,500/month).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social insurance (health, pension, unemployment, accident).
    *   Employer ~21%, employee ~18%.
*   Bonus/13th & 14th Month Pay: Mandatory under CBAs (holiday allowance + Christmas bonus).

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods (Angestelltengesetz):
    *   6 weeks (after 2 years service).
    *   Increases up to 5 months (after 25 years service).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   Under _Abfertigung Neu_ (2003 reform): employer contributes 1.53% of wages to employee’s pension fund.
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal.
*   Employees must contribute to social insurance.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate: Enforces compliance with working time, safety, and wage laws.
*   Works Councils: Strong role in employee representation and workplace decisions.
*   Dispute Resolution: Labour courts handle disputes.

**🇦🇹 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   Collective Bargaining Agreements (CBAs):
    *   Define minimum wages, overtime rates, allowances, and bonuses.
    *   Binding for most industries.
*   Holidays Act (Urlaubsgesetz):
    *   Governs annual leave and public holiday entitlements.
*   Labour Constitution Act (Arbeitsverfassungsgesetz):
    *   Strong role for works councils in employee representation.
*   Data Protection (GDPR):
    *   Employers must comply with EU data privacy rules.
*   Recent Developments:
*   Digital workplace regulations (AI systems require works council approval).
*   Ongoing updates to CBAs adjusting minimum wages and allowances.
