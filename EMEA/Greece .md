**Country Name: Greece**

**🇬🇷 Greece – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Code_ (Law 4808/2021 – modern labour law reform).
    *   _Civil Code_ (general contractual obligations).
    *   _Social Security Law (EFKA system)_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour and Social Affairs.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; collective bargaining agreements (CBAs) supplement statutory rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (must specify duration and justification).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/seasonal.
*   Probationary Periods: Up to 12 months for permanent contracts.
*   Mandatory Clauses: job description, wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Greek is mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed but Greek version prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (8 hours/day).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.2×_ hourly wage → overtime up to 3 hours/day.
    *   _1.4×_ hourly wage → overtime beyond daily limits.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → work on Sundays/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 24 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   20 working days/year (for 5‑day week).
    *   Increases with tenure (up to 25 days).
*   Public Holidays: 12 annually (e.g., Independence Day, Easter, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: Paid; employer covers first 3 days, thereafter social security.
*   Maternity Leave: 17 weeks (paid).
*   Paternity Leave: 14 days (paid).
*   Parental Leave: Up to 4 months unpaid, job protected.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted annually (~€830/month in 2025).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (employer ~22%, employee ~15%).
    *   Health, pension, unemployment.
*   Bonus/13th & 14th Month Pay: Mandatory — Christmas bonus, Easter bonus, and vacation allowance.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   1 month (
    *   Scales up to 4 months (≥12 years service).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   1 month’s wages (1 year service).
    *   Scales up to 12 months (≥16 years service).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate (SEPE): Enforces compliance with labour law.
*   Penalties: Fines for breaches of working time, wage, and safety rules.
*   Dispute Resolution: Labour courts handle disputes.
