**Country Name: Slovakia**

**🇸🇰 Slovakia – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Code (Zákonník práce)_ – core employment framework.
    *   _Social Insurance Act_.
    *   _Occupational Safety and Health Act_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour, Social Affairs and Family.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (max 2 years, renewable twice).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/seasonal work.
*   Probationary Periods:
    *   Max 3 months (regular employees).
    *   Max 6 months (managers).
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Slovak is mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed but Slovak prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (8 hours/day).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.25×_ hourly wage → overtime weekdays.
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → weekends/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 12 hours daily rest; 24 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   4 weeks/year minimum (<33 years old)
    *   5 weeks/year (≥33 years old or parents caring for children).
*   Public Holidays: 15 annually (e.g., Constitution Day, Christmas, Easter).
*   Sick Leave: Paid; employer covers first 10 days, then social insurance.
*   Maternity Leave: 34 weeks (paid at 75% of average wage).
*   Paternity Leave: 2 weeks (paid).
*   Parental Leave: Up to 3 years (job protected, allowance via social insurance).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted annually (~€800/month gross in 2025).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (employer ~35%, employee ~13%).
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common under CBAs.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   1 month (<1 year service)
    *   2 months (≥1 year service).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   1 month’s wages (2–5 years service).
    *   2 months (5–10 years).
    *   3 months (≥10 years).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate: Enforces compliance with labour law.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.
