**Country Name: Israel**

**🇮🇱 Israel – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Hours of Work and Rest Law (1951)_.
    *   _Annual Leave Law (1951)_.
    *   _Employment of Women Law (1954)_.
    *   _National Insurance Law (1995)_.
    *   _Collective Agreements Law (1957)_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labor, Social Affairs and Social Services.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs and extension orders are highly influential.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term.
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/project‑based.
*   Probationary Periods: Not formally regulated; typically 3–6 months by contract.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Hebrew is standard; bilingual contracts allowed but Hebrew prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 42 hours/week (7–8 hours/day).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.25×_ hourly wage → first 2 overtime hours.
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → beyond 2 hours.
*   Rest Periods: 36 consecutive hours weekly rest (Friday–Saturday common).

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   12–28 days depending on tenure.
*   Public Holidays: 9–10 annually (Jewish religious + national).
*   Sick Leave: Paid; first day unpaid, 2nd–3rd day at 50%, thereafter 100%.
*   Maternity Leave: 26 weeks (15 paid by National Insurance, 11 unpaid).
*   Paternity Leave: Fathers may share maternity leave; short paid leave available.
*   Parental Leave: Job protection during maternity/parental leave.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted annually (~ILS 5,880/month in 2025).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   National Insurance (employer ~7.6%, employee ~3.5%).
    *   Pension contributions mandatory (employer ~6.5%, employee ~6%).
*   Recuperation Pay (Dmei Havraa): Annual allowance based on tenure.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   1 day per month of service (first 6 months).
    *   2.5 days per month (7–12 months).
    *   1 month (after 1 year).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   1 month’s wages per year of service (if employed ≥1 year).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to National Insurance and pension.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Ministry of Labor Inspectorate: Enforces compliance with labour law.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   Recent Developments (2026):
    *   Expiration of wartime measures (extra benefits for reservists ended).
    *   New tax credits for combat reservists.
    *   Recuperation pay reverted to full payments.
*   Foreign Workers: Special protections under the _Foreign Workers’ Rights Handbook_.

🇮🇱 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know

*   Hours of Work and Rest Law (1951): Governs working time and weekly rest.
*   Annual Leave Law (1951): Defines minimum vacation entitlements.
*   Employment of Women Law (1954): Protects maternity rights and parental leave.
*   National Insurance Law (1995): Covers health, pension, unemployment, and accident insurance.
*   Collective Agreements Law (1957): Allows CBAs to be extended sector‑wide.
*   Language Requirement: Contracts must be in Hebrew; bilingual versions allowed but Hebrew prevails.
*   Recent Developments (2026):
*   Sick leave and recuperation pay enforcement strengthened.
*   Tax credits for reservists introduced.
*   Foreign workers’ rights handbook updated.
*   Minimum wage adjusted annually.
