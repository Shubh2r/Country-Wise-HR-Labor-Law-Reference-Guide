**Country Name: Spain**

**🇪🇸 Spain – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Workers’ Statute (Estatuto de los Trabajadores)_ – core employment framework.
    *   _Social Security Law_.
    *   _Occupational Risk Prevention Law_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour and Social Economy.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs are highly influential and widely applied.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (only for justified temporary needs).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/seasonal work.
*   Probationary Periods:
    *   Max 6 months (technical staff).
    *   Max 2 months (other employees).
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Spanish is mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed but Spanish prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (average annual).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   Max 80 hours/year.
    *   Paid at _1.75×_ hourly wage or compensated with time off.
*   Rest Periods: 12 hours daily rest; 1.5 days weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   30 calendar days/year minimum (≈22 working days).
*   Public Holidays: 14 annually (national + regional).
*   Sick Leave: Paid via social security after 3 days; employer covers first 3 days in some CBAs.
*   Maternity Leave: 16 weeks (paid at 100% of salary via social security).
*   Paternity Leave: 16 weeks (equal to maternity, paid).
*   Parental Leave: Additional unpaid leave possible; flexible arrangements under CBAs.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage (SMI): Adjusted annually (~€1,134/month gross in 2025).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (employer ~29.9%, employee ~6.35%).
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Mandatory — employees entitled to 2 extra payments per year (often June & December).

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   15 days (general rule).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   Objective dismissal: 20 days’ wages per year of service (max 12 months).
    *   Unfair dismissal: 33 days’ wages per year of service (max 24 months).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation (15 days).
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate: Enforces compliance with labour law.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Extremely influential — often set wages, working hours, and sector‑specific entitlements.

**🇪🇸 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Workers’ Statute (Estatuto de los Trabajadores)**: Governs contracts, leave, termination, and dispute resolution.
*   **Social Security Law**: Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   **Occupational Risk Prevention Law**: Workplace safety standards.
*   **Language Requirement**: Contracts must be in Spanish; bilingual versions allowed but Spanish prevails.
*   **Recent Developments**:
*   Annual minimum wage (SMI) adjustments.
*   Equalization of maternity and paternity leave (both 16 weeks).
*   Stronger enforcement of CBAs in wage setting.
*   EU‑wide obligations (GDPR, digital workplace rules).
