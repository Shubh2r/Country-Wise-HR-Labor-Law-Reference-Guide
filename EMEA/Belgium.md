**Country Name: Belgium**

**🇧🇬 Belgium – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Code (Belgian Employment Law)_.
    *   _Social Security Code_.
    *   _Well-being at Work Act_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Federal Public Service Employment, Labour and Social Dialogue.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; collective agreements supplement statutory rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (up to 3 years, renewable).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/seasonal.
*   Probationary Periods: Up to 6 months; must be specified in contract.
*   Mandatory Clauses: job description, wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Contracts must be in Dutch, French, or German depending on region; bilingual contracts allowed but regional language prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 38 hours/week.
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime on weekdays.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → overtime on weekends/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 35 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   20 working days/year minimum.
    *   Longer entitlements possible under collective agreements.
*   Public Holidays: 10 annually (e.g., Labour Day, National Day, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: Paid; first 30 days covered by employer, thereafter by social security.
*   Maternity Leave: 15 weeks (paid)
*   Paternity Leave: 20 days (paid).
*   Parental Leave: Flexible schemes available until child reaches 12 years.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted annually (~€2,050/month in 2025).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Employer ~25%, employee ~13.07% (social security).
    *   Covers health, pension, unemployment.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in some sectors.
*   **Meal Vouchers**: €10/day (new 2026 reform).

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   2 weeks (short service).
    *   Up to 3 months depending on seniority.
*   Severance Pay:
    *   Based on length of service and collective agreements.
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate: Enforces compliance with labour law.
*   Penalties: Fines for breaches of working time, wage, and safety rules.
*   Dispute Resolution: Labour courts handle disputes.

**🇧🇬 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Labour Code (Кодекс на труда):**
    *   Governs contracts, working conditions, and dispute resolution.
*   **Social Security Code:**
    *   Covers health, pension, unemployment, and accident insurance.
*   **Collective Agreements:**
    *   Can extend leave entitlements, bonuses, and allowances beyond statutory minimums.
*   **Language Requirement:**
    *   Contracts must be in Bulgarian; bilingual versions allowed but Bulgarian prevails.
*   **Recent Developments:**

Minimum wage adjusted annually.

Sick leave rules clarified (first 3 days employer, thereafter social security).

EU‑wide compliance obligations (GDPR, digital workplace rules).
