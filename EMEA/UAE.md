**Country Name: UAE**

**🇦🇪 UAE – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021 on Regulation of Labour Relations_ (effective 2022, with amendments through 2026).
    *   _Cabinet Resolution No. 1 of 2022_ (executive regulations).
    *   _Occupational Health & Safety Regulations_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation (MoHRE).
*   Applicability: Covers private sector employees; free zones may have additional rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Limited-term (fixed-term, max 3 years, renewable).
    *   Unlimited-term contracts phased out in 2023; all contracts now limited-term.
    *   Part-time, temporary, flexible work arrangements recognized.
*   Probationary Periods: Max 6 months.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Arabic mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed but Arabic prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 48 hours/week (8 hours/day, 6 days).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.25×_ hourly wage → overtime weekdays.
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime nights/weekends.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 24 hours weekly rest.
*   Ramadan Hours: Reduced to 6 hours/day.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave (based on tenure):
    *   30 calendar days/year (after 1 year of service).
    *   2 days/month (for 6–12 months service).
*   Public Holidays: 14 annually (e.g., Eid al‑Fitr, Eid al‑Adha, National Day).
*   Sick Leave: 90 days/year (first 15 days full pay, next 30 days half pay, next 45 days unpaid).
*   Maternity Leave: 60 days (45 days full pay, 15 days half pay).
*   Paternity Leave: 5 days (paid).
*   Parental Leave: 5 days (paid, shared).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: No statutory minimum wage; wages set by contract and CBAs.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Emirati nationals: employer contributions to pension fund.
    *   Expatriates: employer must provide end‑of‑service gratuity.
*   End‑of‑Service Gratuity:
    *   21 days’ basic wage per year (first 5 years).
    *   30 days’ basic wage per year (after 5 years).
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in practice.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   30–90 days depending on contract.
*   Severance Pay: End‑of‑service gratuity as above.
*   Exemptions: No gratuity if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Employees must respect UAE labour law and immigration rules.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   MoHRE: Oversees compliance.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   Free Zones: May have additional rules (e.g., DIFC, ADGM).
*   CBAs: Rare, but sectoral agreements exist.

**9\. References**

*   Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation (MoHRE): [https://www.mohre.gov.ae](https://www.mohre.gov.ae/)
*   Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021 – via UAE legislation portal.
*   Cabinet Resolution No. 1 of 2022 – executive regulations.

**🇦🇪 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Federal Decree‑Law No. 33 of 2021**: Governs contracts, leave, termination.
*   **Cabinet Resolution No. 1 of 2022**: Executive regulations.
*   **Occupational Health & Safety Regulations**: Workplace safety standards.
*   **Language Requirement**: Contracts must be in Arabic; bilingual versions allowed but Arabic prevails.
*   **Recent Developments**:
*   Emiratization quotas expanded — mandatory hiring of UAE nationals in certain sectors.
*   Ongoing reforms to end‑of‑service gratuity (possible shift to savings schemes).
*   Free zone rules (DIFC, ADGM, JAFZA) may diverge from federal law.
