**Country Name: Netherland**

**🇳🇱 Netherlands – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Dutch Civil Code (Burgerlijk Wetboek)_ – Book 7 governs employment contracts.
    *   _Working Hours Act (Arbeidstijdenwet)_.
    *   _Work and Care Act (Wet arbeid en zorg)_.
    *   _Collective Labour Agreements (CAOs)_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Social Affairs and Employment (SZW).
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CAOs are binding and widely applied.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (max 3 years, after which permanent unless justified).
    *   On‑call/zero‑hours contracts.
    *   Agency work.
*   Probationary Periods:
    *   Max 2 months (permanent contracts).
    *   1 month (fixed‑term
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Dutch is standard; bilingual contracts allowed but Dutch prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (average).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   No statutory overtime rate; determined by contract or CAO.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 36 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   20 working days/year minimum (for full‑time).
    *   CAOs often grant 25–30 days.
*   Public Holidays: 10 annually (e.g., King’s Day, Liberation Day, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: Paid; employer covers at least 70% of wages for up to 2 years.
*   Maternity Leave: 16 weeks (paid).
*   Paternity Leave: 1 week (paid), plus 5 weeks unpaid but compensated via social insurance.
*   Parental Leave: Up to 26 weeks (partially paid since 2022 reforms).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted biannually (~€2,100/month gross in 2025 for full‑time).
*   Overtime Pay: As per contract/CAO.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (employer ~18%, employee ~27%).
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, disability.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common under CAOs.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   1 month (<5 years Service )
    *   2 months (5–10 years).
    *   3 months (10–15 years).
    *   4 months (≥15 years).
*   Severance Pay (Transition Payment):
    *   1/3 month’s salary per year of service.
    *   Capped at ~€90,000 or 1 year’s salary (whichever is higher).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate (Inspectie SZW): Enforces compliance with labour law.
*   Labour Courts (Kantonrechter): Handle disputes.
*   CAOs: Binding and enforceable across industries.

**🇳🇱 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Dutch Civil Code (Book 7)**: Governs contracts, dismissal, and employee rights.
*   **Working Hours Act**: Defines maximum hours, rest breaks, and night work rules.
*   **Work and Care Act**: Governs maternity, paternity, parental, and care leave.
*   **Collective Labour Agreements (CAOs)**: Define wages, bonuses, overtime, and leave beyond statutory minimums.
*   **Language Requirement**: Contracts must be in Dutch; bilingual versions allowed but Dutch prevails.
*   **Recent Developments**:
*   Biannual minimum wage adjustments.
*   Expansion of parental leave (partially paid since 2022).
*   EU‑wide compliance obligations (GDPR, AI workplace rules).
