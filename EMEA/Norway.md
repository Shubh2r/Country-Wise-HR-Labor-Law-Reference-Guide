**Country Name: Norway**

**🇳🇴 Norway – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Working Environment Act (Arbeidsmiljøloven)_ – cornerstone of employment law.
    *   _Holiday Act_ – annual leave entitlements.
    *   _National Insurance Act_ – social security.
*   Regulatory Authority: Norwegian Labour Inspection Authority.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs are highly influential.
*   Recent Developments (2025–26):
*   Continued focus on employee well‑being and work‑life balance.
*   Digitalisation of payroll and compliance systems.
*   Strengthened data protection and employee privacy rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment: Permanent, fixed‑term (only if justified), temporary.
*   Probationary Periods: Up to 6 months.
*   Mandatory Clauses: job description, wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Norwegian or English.
*   Written Contracts: Mandatory for all employees.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (normal); CBAs often reduce to 37.5 hours.
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   Limited to 200 hours/year.
    *   Paid at _1.4×_ hourly wage minimum.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 35 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave: 25 working days/year (Holiday Act).
*   Public Holidays: ~10 annually (e.g., Constitution Day, Christmas, Easter).
*   Sick Leave: 52 weeks (paid via National Insurance; employer covers first 16 days).
*   Parental Leave: 12 months shared between parents (paid).
*   Maternity Leave: 3 weeks before and 6 weeks after birth mandatory.
*   Paternity Leave: 2 weeks mandatory; part of shared parental quota.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: No statutory minimum; wages set by CBAs.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   National Insurance: employer ~14.1%, employee ~8.2%.
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in CBAs.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   **Notice Periods:**
    *   1 month (< 5 years of service)
    *   2 months (≥5 years).
    *   3 months (≥10 years).
    *   Longer for employees over 50.
*   Severance Pay: Not statutory; negotiated in CBAs or contracts.
*   Termination Rules: Must be based on documented cause; formal consultation required.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Employees must contribute to National Insurance.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspection Authority: Oversees compliance.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.
