**Country Name: Italy**

**🇮🇹 Italy – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

**•** Primary Laws:

• Italian Civil Code (employment contracts).

• Workers’ Statute (Statuto dei Lavoratori, Law No. 300/1970).

• Jobs Act (2015 reforms).

• National Collective Bargaining Agreements (CCNLs).

• Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour and Social Policies.

• Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs are binding and highly influential.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

**•** Types of Employment:

• Permanent (open‑ended).

• Fixed‑term (max 24 months, renewable within limits).

• Apprenticeship contracts.

• Part‑time.

• Temporary/agency work.

• Probationary Periods: Typically 6 months (varies by CCNL).

• Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.

• Language Requirement: Italian is mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed but Italian prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

**•** Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (average).

• Overtime Rules:

• 1.25× hourly wage → overtime weekdays.

• 1.5× hourly wage → overtime weekends/public holidays.

• Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 24 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

**•** Annual Leave:

• Minimum 4 weeks/year.

• CBAs often grant 5+ weeks.

• Public Holidays: 12 annually (e.g., Liberation Day, Ferragosto, Christmas).

• Sick Leave: Paid; employer covers part, social security covers remainder.

• Maternity Leave: 5 months (2 before, 3 after birth, paid at 80%).

• Paternity Leave: 10 days (paid).

• Parental Leave: Up to 6 months per parent (job protected, allowance available).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

**•** Minimum Wage: No statutory minimum; set by CBAs (~€1,000–€1,200/month typical).

• Overtime Pay: As above.

• Mandatory Contributions:

• Social security (employer ~30%, employee ~10%).

• Covers pension, health, unemployment, maternity.

• Bonus/13th & 14th Month Pay: Mandatory under most CBAs (Christmas bonus + summer bonus).

**6\. Termination & Severance**

**•** Notice Periods:

• Vary by CCNL and tenure (typically 15 days–6 months).

• Severance Pay (TFR – Trattamento di Fine Rapporto):

• Mandatory lump sum accrued annually (~1/13.5 of annual salary).

• Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

**•** Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.

• Must give contractual notice for resignation.

• Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.

• Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

**•** Labour Inspectorate (INL): Enforces compliance with labour law.

• Labour Courts (Tribunali del Lavoro): Handle disputes.

• CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.

**9\. References**

**•** Ministry of Labour and Social Policies (Italy): https://www.lavoro.gov.it

• INPS (National Social Security Institute): https://www.inps.it

• Italian Civil Code & Labour Statute – via Italian legislation portal.

*   **Workers’ Statute (Law No. 300/1970)**: Protects union rights, workplace freedoms.
*   **Jobs Act (2015 reforms)**: Modernized dismissal rules, introduced new contract types.
*   **Collective Bargaining Agreements (CCNLs)**: Define wages, bonuses, overtime, and leave beyond statutory minimums.
*   **Language Requirement**: Contracts must be in Italian; bilingual versions allowed but Italian prevails.
*   **Recent Developments**:
*   Ongoing debate on statutory minimum wage introduction.
*   Expansion of parental leave flexibility.
*   EU‑wide compliance obligations (GDPR, AI workplace rules).
