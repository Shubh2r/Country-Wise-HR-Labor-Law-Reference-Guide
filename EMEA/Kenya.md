**Country Name: Kenya**

**🇰🇪 Kenya – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Employment Act (2007)_ – core employment framework.
    *   _Labour Relations Act (2007)_.
    *   _Occupational Safety and Health Act (2007)_.
    *   _Work Injury Benefits Act (2007)_.
    *   _National Social Security Fund Act (2013)_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour and Social Protection.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term.
    *   Casual (daily/seasonal).
    *   Apprenticeship/trainee.
*   Probationary Periods: Up to 6 months (extendable to 12 months by agreement).
*   Mandatory Clauses: job description, wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: English or Kiswahili; must be clear and comprehensible.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 52 hours/week maximum (varies by sector).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime weekdays.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → overtime on rest days/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 1 day weekly rest (Sunday common).

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   21 working days/year minimum (after 12 months service).
*   Public Holidays: 12 annually (e.g., Madaraka Day, Mashujaa Day, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: 7 days full pay + 7 days half pay per year (after 2 months service).
*   Maternity Leave: 3 months (paid).
*   Paternity Leave: 2 weeks (paid).
*   Parental Leave: Job protected; adoption leave available (2 months).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Set annually by Wage Orders; varies by sector/location (~KES 15,000–20,000/month in 2025 for urban areas).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   National Social Security Fund (NSSF).
    *   National Hospital Insurance Fund (NHIF).
    *   PAYE income tax.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in some industries.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   28 days (monthly contracts).
    *   7 days (weekly contracts).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   15 days’ wages per year of service (for redundancy).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to NSSF and NHIF.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate (Ministry of Labour): Enforces compliance with labour law.
*   Penalties: Fines for breaches of working time, wage, and safety rules.
*   Dispute Resolution: Employment and Labour Relations Court handles disputes.
