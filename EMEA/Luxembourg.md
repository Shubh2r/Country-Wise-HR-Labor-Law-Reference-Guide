**Country Name: Luxembourg**

**🇱🇺 Luxembourg – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Code (Code du Travail)_.
    *   _Social Security Code_.
    *   _Collective Bargaining Agreements (CBAs)_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour, Employment and the Social and Solidarity Economy.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (max 24 months, renewable within limits).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/agency work.
*   Probationary Periods: 2–12 months depending on role.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: French is standard; bilingual contracts allowed but French prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week.
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → Sundays/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 44 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   26 working days/year minimum.
*   Public Holidays: 11 annually (e.g., National Day, Assumption, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: Paid; employer covers first 77 days, then social security.
*   Maternity Leave: 20 weeks (paid).
*   Paternity Leave: 10 days (paid).
*   Parental Leave: Up to 6 months full‑time or 12 months part‑time (job protected).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted annually (~€2,570/month gross in 2025 for unskilled workers; ~€3,085/month for skilled workers).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (employer ~12%, employee ~12%).
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common under CBAs.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   2 months (<5 Years)
    *   4 months (5–10 years).
    *   6 months (≥10 years).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   1 month’s wages (≥5 years service).
    *   Scales up to 12 months (≥30 years service).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate (ITM – Inspection du Travail et des Mines): Enforces compliance with labour law.
*   Labour Courts (Tribunaux du Travail): Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.

*   **Labour Code (Code du Travail)**: Governs contracts, working conditions, and dispute resolution.
*   **Collective Bargaining Agreements (CBAs)**: Define sector‑specific wages, bonuses, and entitlements.
*   **Social Security Code**: Covers health, pension, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   **Language Requirement**: Contracts must be in French; bilingual versions allowed but French prevails.
