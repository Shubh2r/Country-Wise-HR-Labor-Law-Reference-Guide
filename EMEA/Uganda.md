**Country Name: Uganda**

**🇺🇬 Uganda – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Employment Act, 2006 (Chapter 226)_ – contracts, rights, termination.
    *   _Occupational Safety and Health Act, 2006_ (amended in 2025).
    *   _Labour Unions Act, 2006_.
    *   _Workers’ Compensation Act_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Gender, Labour and Social Development.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.
*   Recent Developments (2023–25):
*   _Employment Amendment Bill No. 2 of 2022_ passed in 2023, modernizing employment rules.
*   _Occupational Safety and Health Amendment Bill 2023_ passed in 2025, updating workplace safety.
*   _Public Service Pension Fund Bill 2024_ strengthened social protection.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (allowed if justified).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/seasonal work.
*   Probationary Periods: Max 6 months.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: English is standard.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 48 hours/week (8 hours/day, 6 days).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime weekdays.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 24 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   21 working days/year minimum.
*   Public Holidays: 12 annually (e.g., Independence Day, Christmas, Easter, Eid).
*   Sick Leave: 30 days/year (paid).
*   Maternity Leave: 60 working days (paid).
*   Paternity Leave: 4 working days (paid).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Currently under review; system considered outdated, reforms expected.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   National Social Security Fund (NSSF): employer 10%, employee 5%.
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in practice**.**

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   2 weeks (6 months–1 year service).
    *   1 month (1–5 years).
    *   2 months (5–10 years).
    *   3 months (≥10 years).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   Required after 6 months of continuous service if termination is due to redundancy or unfair dismissal.
    *   Formula: 2 weeks’ wages per year of service (minimum entitlement).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Employees must contribute to NSSF.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate: Oversees compliance.
*   Industrial Court: Handles disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.
