**Country Name: Sweden**

**🇸🇪 Sweden – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Employment Protection Act (LAS)_ – contracts, termination.
    *   _Working Hours Act_.
    *   _Annual Leave Act_.
    *   _Discrimination Act_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Swedish Work Environment Authority, Ministry of Employment.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs are extremely influential and often override statutory minimums.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (default).
    *   Fixed‑term (limited grounds, max 2 years).
    *   Probationary (max 6 months).
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, duties, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Swedish is standard; bilingual contracts allowed.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week.
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime weekdays.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → weekends/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 36 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   25 working days/year minimum.
*   Public Holidays: 13 annually (e.g., Midsummer, Christmas, National Day).
*   Sick Leave: Employer pays 80% for first 14 days; then social insurance.
*   Maternity/Paternity Leave: 480 days parental leave shared between parents (paid via social insurance, 390 days at ~80% pay, 90 days flat rate).
*   Recent EU Directive Implementation (2026): Pay Transparency Directive — employers must provide pay information, cannot ask applicants about previous salaries, and must inform employees about pay rules.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: No statutory minimum; wages set by CBAs.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Employer social security ~31.42%.
    *   Employee income tax (progressive).
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common under CBAs.
*   2026 Update: Strengthened in‑work tax credit, reducing tax burden by ~SEK 400/month for average earners.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods (LAS):
    *   1 month (<2 years service)
    *   2 months (2–4 years).
    *   3 months (4–6 years).
    *   4 months (6–8 years).
    *   5 months (8–10 years).
    *   6 months (≥10 years).
*   Severance Pay: Not statutory; CBAs may provide redundancy compensation.
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Employees must contribute to income tax and social insurance.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Swedish Work Environment Authority: Enforces workplace safety.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.

**🇸🇪 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Employment Protection Act (LAS)**: Governs contracts, notice, termination.
*   **Working Hours Act**: Sets limits on hours and rest.
*   **Annual Leave Act**: Guarantees 25 days.
*   **Discrimination Act**: Equality and non‑discrimination.
*   **Language Requirement**: Contracts typically in Swedish; bilingual versions allowed.
*   **Recent Developments (2026)**:
*   Retirement age raised to 67, linked to life expectancy.
*   EU Pay Transparency Directive implemented — employers must disclose pay structures, cannot ask applicants about past salaries, and must provide pay information to employees.
*   Strengthened in‑work tax credit (~SEK 400/month relief for average earners).
