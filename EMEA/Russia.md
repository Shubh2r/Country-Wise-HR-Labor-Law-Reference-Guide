**Country Name: Russia**

**🇷🇺 Russia – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Code of the Russian Federation (2002)_ – core employment framework.
    *   _Social Insurance Law_.
    *   _Trade Union Law_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour and Social Protection.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.
*   Context (2026): Despite sanctions and geopolitical pressures, Russia maintains a large domestic labour market with evolving compliance requirements.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (up to 5 years).
    *   Seasonal (up to 6 months).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/agency work.
*   Probationary Periods:
    *   Max 3 months (regular employees).
    *   Max 6 months (executives/managers).
*   Mandatory Clauses: job title, duties, salary (in RUB), working hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Russian is mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed but Russian prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (8 hours/day).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → first 2 hours overtime.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → subsequent hours, weekends, public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 42 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   28 calendar days/year minimum.
*   Public Holidays: 14 annually (e.g., New Year, Victory Day, Russia Day).
*   Sick Leave: Paid via social insurance (rate depends on tenure).
*   Maternity Leave: 140 days (70 before, 70 after birth, paid).
*   Paternity Leave: Short leave available; broader parental leave rights apply.
*   Parental Leave: Up to 3 years (job protected, partially paid).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: ~19,242 RUB/month in 2024; adjusted annually.
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social insurance (employer ~30%).
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in practice.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   2 weeks (employee resignation).
    *   Employer notice varies by contract type; seasonal employees 7 days.
*   Severance Pay:
    *   2 weeks’ average earnings (general redundancy).
    *   1 month’s wages (mass layoffs).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to social insurance.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate (Rostrud): Enforces compliance with labour law.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.
