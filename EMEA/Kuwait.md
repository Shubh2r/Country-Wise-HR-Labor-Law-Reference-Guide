**Country Name: Kuwait**

**🇰🇼 Kuwait – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Kuwait Labour Law No. 6 of 2010_ (amended 2015, 2020).
    *   _Private Sector Labour Law_.
    *   _Social Security Law_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Social Affairs and Labour.
*   Applicability: Covers private sector employees; public sector governed separately.
*   Recent Developments (2025–26):
*   Strengthened wage protection system.
*   Reforms to expatriate worker rights.
*   Enhanced occupational safety standards.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment: Permanent, fixed‑term, temporary.
*   Probationary Periods: Up to 100 days.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Arabic mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed.
*   Written Contracts: Required for all employees.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week.
*   Overtime Rules: Paid at _1.25×_ hourly wage (daytime), _1.5×_ (night work).
*   Rest Periods: Weekly rest day (Friday).
*   Summer Midday Work Ban: No outdoor work between 11:00–16:00 during peak summer months.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   30 days/year (after 1 year of service).
*   Public Holidays: ~13 annually (Eid al‑Fitr, Eid al‑Adha, National Day, Liberation Day).
*   Sick Leave: 15 days full pay, 10 days half pay, then unpaid.
*   Maternity Leave: 70 days (paid).
*   Paternity Leave: Not statutory, but some employers provide 3–5 days.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: KWD 75/month (private sector baseline).
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social Security for Kuwaiti nationals (pension fund).
    *   Expatriates covered by employer‑provided insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in practice.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   3 months (monthly paid employees).
    *   1 month (daily/weekly paid employees).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   15 days’ wages per year of service (
    *   1 month’s wages per year of service (≥5 years).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Expatriates must maintain valid residency permits.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Ministry of Social Affairs and Labour: Oversees compliance.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   Wage Protection System (WPS): Mandatory electronic salary transfer.
*   CBAs: Rare, but possible in certain sectors.
