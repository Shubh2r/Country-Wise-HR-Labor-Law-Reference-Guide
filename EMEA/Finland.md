**Country Name: Finland**

**🇫🇮 Finland – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Employment Contracts Act (2001)_ – contracts, rights, termination.
    *   _Working Hours Act (2019)_ – hours, overtime, rest.
    *   _Annual Holidays Act (2005)_ – leave entitlements.
    *   _Occupational Safety and Health Act (2002)_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Economic Affairs and Employment; Regional State Administrative Agencies.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs are highly influential and often override statutory minimums.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (default).
    *   Fixed‑term (must be justified).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/seasonal work.
*   Probationary Periods: Max 6 months (extended to 12 months if training is required).
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Finnish or Swedish mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (8 hours/day).
*   Flexible Working: Employees can request flexible arrangements under the Working Hours Act.
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → first overtime hours.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → subsequent hours.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 35 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   2 days/month worked (first year).
    *   2.5 days/month worked (after 1 year).
    *   Typically 24–30 days/year.
*   Public Holidays: 12 annually (e.g., Independence Day, Christmas, Easter, Midsummer).
*   Sick Leave: Employer pays full salary for first 9 days; then social insurance (Kela) covers.
*   Maternity Leave: 105 working days (paid).
*   Paternity Leave: 54 working days (paid).
*   Parental Leave: Flexible system allowing parents to share ~320 days of leave.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: No statutory minimum; wages set by CBAs.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (employer ~20–25%, employee ~7%).
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common under CBAs.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods (based on tenure):
    *   14 days (< 1 years of service)
    *   1 month (1–4 years).
    *   2 months (4–8 years).
    *   4 months (8–12 years).
    *   6 months (≥12 years).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   No statutory severance; employees entitled to notice pay.
    *   CBAs may provide additional severance benefits.
*   Exemptions: No notice if dismissed for serious misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Regional State Administrative Agencies: Enforce labour law.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.
