**Country Name: UK**

**🇬🇧 United Kingdom – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Employment Rights Act 1996_ – contracts, termination, redundancy.
    *   _Working Time Regulations 1998_ – hours, rest, holidays.
    *   _Equality Act 2010_ – non‑discrimination.
    *   _Health and Safety at Work Act 1974_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Department for Business and Trade; Advisory, Conciliation and Arbitration Service (ACAS).
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term.
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Zero‑hours contracts (legal but regulated).
*   Probationary Periods: Not legally defined; typically 3–6 months.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: English is standard; bilingual contracts allowed.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 48 hours/week maximum (averaged over 17 weeks).
*   Opt‑Out: Employees may voluntarily opt out of the 48‑hour cap.
*   Overtime Rules: No statutory premium; rates set by contract or CBAs.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 24 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   28 days/year (including public holidays) for full‑time employees.
*   Public Holidays: 8 annually (England & Wales; Scotland and Northern Ireland differ).
*   Sick Leave: Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) up to 28 weeks (£116.75/week in 2025).
*   Maternity Leave: 52 weeks (39 weeks paid: 6 weeks at 90% pay, then statutory maternity pay).
*   Paternity Leave: 2 weeks (paid at statutory rate).
*   Shared Parental Leave: Up to 50 weeks shared between parents.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage (National Minimum & Living Wage): Adjusted annually (~£11.44/hour for workers ≥23 in 2025).
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   National Insurance (employer ~13.8%, employee ~12%).
    *   Pension auto‑enrolment contributions (employer min 3%, employee min 5%).
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in practice.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   1 week (≥1 month service, < 2 years service)
    *   1 week per year of service (2–12 years).
    *   Max 12 weeks (≥12 years).
*   Redundancy Pay:
    *   0.5 week’s pay per year (<22 Years old)
    *   1 week’s pay per year (22–41 years).
    *   1.5 week’s pay per year (≥41 years).
    *   Capped at 20 years’ service and statutory weekly pay ceiling.
*   Exemptions: No redundancy pay if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Employees must contribute to National Insurance and pensions.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   ACAS: Provides guidance and mediation.
*   Employment Tribunals: Handle disputes.
*   Health and Safety Executive (HSE): Enforces workplace safety.
*   CBAs: Binding in unionized sectors.
