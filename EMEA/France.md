**Country Name: France**

**🇫🇷 France – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

**•** Primary Laws:

• French Labour Code (Code du Travail) – core employment framework.

• Social Security Code.

• Civil Code (general contractual obligations).

• Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour, Employment and Economic Inclusion.

• Applicability: Covers all employees; collective bargaining agreements (CBAs) are highly influential.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

**•** Types of Employment:

• Permanent (CDI – contrat à durée indéterminée).

• Fixed‑term (CDD – contrat à durée déterminée).

• Temporary/agency work.

• Apprenticeship contracts.

• Probationary Periods:

• 2–4 months depending on role; renewable once if agreed.

• Mandatory Clauses: job description, wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.

• Language Requirement: Contracts must be in French; bilingual versions allowed but French prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

**•** Standard Hours: 35 hours/week (legal threshold).

• Overtime Rules:

• 1.25× hourly wage → first 8 overtime hours (36–43 hours).

• 1.5× hourly wage → beyond 43 hours.

• Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 35 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

**•** Annual Leave:

• 5 weeks/year minimum.

• Public Holidays: 11 annually (e.g., Bastille Day, Christmas).

• Sick Leave: Paid; employer covers part, then social security.

• Maternity Leave: 16 weeks (6 before, 10 after birth).

• Paternity Leave: 25 days (paid).

• Parental Leave: Up to 3 years (job protected, allowance available).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

**•** Minimum Wage (SMIC): Adjusted annually (~€1,766/month gross in 2025).

• Overtime Pay: As above.

• Mandatory Contributions:

• Social security (employer ~45%, employee ~20%).

• Health, pension, unemployment, family allowances.

• Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common under CBAs.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

**•** Notice Periods:

• 1 month (employees with <2 years service).

• 2 months (≥2 years service).

• Severance Pay:

• 1/4 month’s salary per year (first 10 years).

• 1/3 month’s salary per year thereafter.

• Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

**•** Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.

• Must give contractual notice for resignation.

• Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.

• Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

**•** Labour Inspectorate (Inspection du Travail): Enforces compliance with labour law.

• Penalties: Fines for breaches of working time, wage, and safety rules.

• Dispute Resolution: Labour courts (Conseil de prud’hommes) handle disputes.
