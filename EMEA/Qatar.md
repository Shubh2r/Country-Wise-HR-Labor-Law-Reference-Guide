**Country Name: Qatar**

**🇶🇦 Qatar – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Law No. 14 of 2004_ (amended 2017, 2020, 2022).
    *   _Law No. 21 of 2015_ regulating entry, exit, and residency of expatriates (abolished the Kafala system).
    *   _Domestic Workers Law, 2017_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees except military, police, and domestic workers (separate law).
*   Recent Developments (2025–26):
*   Strengthened wage protection system (WPS).
*   Expanded occupational safety standards.
*   Continued reforms to expatriate worker rights.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment: Permanent, fixed‑term, temporary.
*   Probationary Periods: Up to 6 months.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Arabic mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed.
*   Written Contracts: Required for all employees.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week.
*   Overtime Rules: Paid at _1.25×_ hourly wage (daytime), _1.5×_ (night work).
*   Rest Periods: Weekly rest day (Friday).
*   Summer Midday Work Ban: No outdoor work between 10:00–15:30 during June–August.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   3 weeks/year (<5 years of Service)
    *   4 weeks/year (≥5 years service).
*   Public Holidays: ~10 annually (Eid al‑Fitr, Eid al‑Adha, National Day, Independence Day).
*   Sick Leave: 2 weeks full pay, 2 weeks half pay, then unpaid.
*   Maternity Leave: 7 weeks (paid).
*   Paternity Leave: Not statutory, but some employers provide 3–5 days.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: QAR 1,000/month (introduced 2021, remains in force).
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social insurance for Qatari nationals (pension fund).
    *   Expatriates covered by employer‑provided insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in practice.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   1 month (<2 years of service)
    *   2 months (≥2 years service).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   3 weeks’ wages per year of service.
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Expatriates must maintain valid residency permits.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Ministry of Labour: Oversees compliance.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   Wage Protection System (WPS): Mandatory electronic salary transfer.
*   CBAs: Rare, but possible in certain sectors.
