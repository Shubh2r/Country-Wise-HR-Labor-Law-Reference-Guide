**Country Name: Senegal**

**🇸🇳 Senegal – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Code (Law No. 97‑17 of Dec. 1, 1997)_ – contracts, working hours, termination.
    *   _Social Security Code_.
    *   _Occupational Safety and Health Regulations_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour, Social Dialogue, Professional Organizations, and Relations with Institutions.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.
*   Recent Development (2025–26): Comprehensive Labour Code reform underway to modernize rules, strengthen worker protections, and align with international standards.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (allowed if justified, renewable).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/seasonal work.
*   Probationary Periods: Typically 3–6 months depending on role.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: French mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed but French prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40–48 hours/week depending on sector.
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.25×_ hourly wage → overtime weekdays.
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → weekends/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 24 hours weekly rest (Sunday common).

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   24 working days/year minimum.
*   Public Holidays: 10–12 annually (e.g., Independence Day, Tabaski, Korité, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: Paid via social security; employer covers initial period.
*   Maternity Leave: 14 weeks (paid at 100%).
*   Paternity Leave: 10 days (paid).
*   Parental Leave: Additional unpaid leave possible.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage (SMIG): Adjusted periodically (~CFA 64,000/month in 2025).
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (employer ~7%, employee ~5%).
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common under CBAs.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   15 days–3 months depending on tenure and role.
*   Severance Pay:
    *   25–40% of monthly wage per year of service (depending on tenure).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate: Enforces compliance.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.
*   Reform Trend: Labour inspectors expected to gain stronger enforcement powers.
