**Country Name: Czech Republic**

**🇨🇿 Czech Republic – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Code (Act No. 262/2006 Coll.)_ – core employment law.
    *   _Employment Act (Act No. 435/2004 Coll.)_.
    *   _Social Security Act_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour and Social Affairs (MPSV).
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; collective agreements supplement statutory rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (maximum 3 years, renewable up to 3 times).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/agency work.
*   Probationary Periods: Up to 3 months (6 months for managerial staff).
*   Mandatory Clauses: job description, wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Czech is mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed but Czech version prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (8 hours/day).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.25×_ hourly wage → overtime.
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime on weekends/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 35 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   4 weeks/year minimum (5 weeks in public sector).
*   Public Holidays: 13 annually (e.g., New Year’s Day, Easter Monday, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: Paid; first 14 days covered by employer, thereafter by social security.
*   Maternity Leave: 28 weeks (37 weeks for multiple births).
*   Paternity Leave: 2 weeks (paid).
*   Parental Leave: Up to 3 years (job protected, allowance available).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Set annually (~CZK 19,500/month in 2025).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (employer ~24.8%, employee ~6.5%).
    *   Health insurance (employer ~9%, employee ~4.5%).
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in some industries.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   2 months (standard).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   1 month’s wages (employment 1–2 years).
    *   2 months’ wages (employment 2–5 years).
    *   3 months’ wages (employment 5+ years).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation (2 months).
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to social security and health insurance.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate (SÚIP): Enforces compliance with labour law.
*   Penalties: Fines for breaches of working time, wage, and safety rules.
*   Dispute Resolution: Labour courts handle disputes.

**9\. References**

*   Ministry of Labour and Social Affairs (MPSV): [https://www.mpsv.cz](https://www.mpsv.cz/)

*   Labour Code (Act No. 262/2006 Coll.):
    *   Governs contracts, working conditions, and dispute resolution.

    *   Regulates employment services, recruitment, and unemployment benefits.
*   Social Security Act:
    *   Employer contributions ~24.8%, employee ~6.5%.
    *   Health insurance employer ~9%, employee ~4.5%.
*   Collective Agreements:
    *   Can extend leave entitlements, bonuses, and allowances beyond statutory minimums.
*   Language Requirement:
    *   Contracts must be in Czech; bilingual versions allowed but Czech prevails.
*   Recent Developments:
*   Minimum wage adjusted annually.
