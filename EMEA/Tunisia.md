**Country Name: Tunisia**

**🇹🇳 Tunisia – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Code (Code du Travail)_ – core employment framework.
    *   _Social Security Code_.
    *   _Occupational Safety and Health Regulations_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Social Affairs.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (max 4 years, renewable).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Seasonal/temporary work.
*   Probationary Periods: Max 6 months.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Arabic or French; bilingual contracts allowed.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 48 hours/week (commonly reduced to 40 in many sectors).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.25×_ hourly wage → overtime weekdays.
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → weekends/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 24 hours weekly rest (Friday or Sunday common).

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   18 working days/year minimum.
    *   Increases with tenure (up to 30 days).
*   Public Holidays: 9–12 annually (e.g., Independence Day, Revolution Day, Eid al‑Fitr, Eid al‑Adha).
*   Sick Leave: Paid via social security; employer covers initial period.
*   Maternity Leave: 30 days (paid at 100%).
*   Paternity Leave: 2 days (paid).
*   Parental Leave: Additional unpaid leave possible.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage (SMIG): Adjusted annually (~TND 429/month in 2025 for 48‑hour week).
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (employer ~16.57%, employee ~9.18%).
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common under CBAs.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   1 month (general rule).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   1 day’s wages per month of service (capped).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate (Ministry of Social Affairs): Enforces compliance.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.
