**Country Name: Germany**

**🇩🇪 Germany – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

**•** Primary Laws:

• German Civil Code (BGB) – contracts.

• Works Constitution Act (Betriebsverfassungsgesetz) – works councils.

• Working Time Act (Arbeitszeitgesetz) – hours and rest.

• Federal Vacation Act (Bundesurlaubsgesetz) – leave.

• Dismissal Protection Act (Kündigungsschutzgesetz) – termination.

• Regulatory Authority: Federal Ministry of Labour and Social Affairs (BMAS).

• Applicability: Covers all employees; collective bargaining agreements (CBAs) are highly influential.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

**•** Types of Employment:

• Permanent (open‑ended).

• Fixed‑term (up to 2 years, renewable twice).

• Part‑time.

• Temporary/agency work.

• Probationary Periods: Up to 6 months.

• Mandatory Clauses: job description, wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.

• Language Requirement: German is standard; bilingual contracts allowed but German version prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

**•** Standard Hours: 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week maximum.

• Overtime Rules:

• Rates defined by CBAs or contracts (commonly 1.25×–1.5×).

• Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; Sunday is statutory rest day.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

**•** Annual Leave (Bundesurlaubsgesetz):

• 20 working days/year minimum (for 5‑day week).

• Many CBAs grant 25–30 days.

• Public Holidays: 9–13 annually depending on state (e.g., Christmas, Unity Day).

• Sick Leave: Paid; employer covers first 6 weeks, then statutory health insurance.

• Maternity Leave: 14 weeks (6 before, 8 after birth).

• Paternity Leave: Not statutory, but fathers can take parental leave.

• Parental Leave: Up to 3 years (job protected, allowance available).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

**•** Minimum Wage: Adjusted annually (~€12.41/hour in 2025).

• Overtime Pay: As per CBAs or contracts.

• Mandatory Contributions:

• Social security (employer ~20.7%, employee ~20.7%).

• Health, pension, unemployment, long‑term care insurance.

• Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common under CBAs.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

**•** Notice Periods (BGB):

• 4 weeks (to 15th or end of month).

• Scales up to 7 months (after 20 years service).

• Severance Pay:

• Not automatic; typically 0.5 month’s salary per year of service if agreed or ordered by court.

• Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

• Dismissal Protection Act: Strong protection for employees in companies with >10 staff.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

**•** Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.

• Must give contractual notice for resignation.

• Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.

• Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

**•** Labour Inspectorate (BMAS & state authorities): Enforces compliance with labour law.

• Works Councils: Strong role in employee representation and workplace decisions.

• Dispute Resolution: Labour courts (Arbeitsgerichte) handle disputes.

**9\. References**

**•** Federal Ministry of Labour and Social Affairs (BMAS): https://www.bmas.de

• German Labour Code (Arbeitsrecht portal): https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de

• German Trade Union Confederation (DGB): [https://www.dgb.de](https://www.dgb.de)
