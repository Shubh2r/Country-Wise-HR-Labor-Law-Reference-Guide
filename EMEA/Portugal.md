**Country Name: Portugal**

**🇵🇹 Portugal – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

**•** Primary Laws:

• Labour Code (Código do Trabalho) – contracts, working hours, termination.

• Social Security Code.

• Occupational Safety and Health Regulations.

• Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour, Solidarity and Social Security.

• Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

**•** Types of Employment:

• Permanent (open‑ended).

• Fixed‑term (max 2 years, renewable).

• Part‑time.

• Temporary/seasonal work.

• Probationary Periods:

• 90 days (general).

• 180 days (roles of trust/complexity).

• 240 days (senior management).

• Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.

• Language Requirement: Portuguese mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed but Portuguese prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

**•** Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (8 hours/day).

• Overtime Rules:

• 1.25× hourly wage → first overtime hour.

• 1.37× hourly wage → subsequent hours.

• 1.5× hourly wage → weekends/public holidays.

• Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 24 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

**•** Annual Leave:

• 22 working days/year minimum.

• Public Holidays: 13 annually (e.g., Freedom Day, Republic Day, Christmas).

• Sick Leave: Paid via social security (after 3rd day).

• Maternity Leave: 120–150 days (paid at 100%).

• Paternity Leave: 20 working days (paid).

• Parental Leave: Shared leave options available.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

**•** Minimum Wage: Adjusted annually (~€820/month in 2025).

• Mandatory Contributions:

• Social security (employer ~23.75%, employee ~11%).

• Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.

• Bonus/13th & 14th Month Pay: Mandatory — paid in June and December.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

**•** Notice Periods (based on tenure):

• 15 days (<1 year service).

• 30 days (1–5 years).

• 60 days (≥5 years).

• Severance Pay:

• 12 days’ base wage per year of service (for redundancy or collective dismissal).

• Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

**•** Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.

• Must give contractual notice for resignation.

• Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

**•** Authority for Working Conditions (ACT): Enforces compliance.

• Labour Courts: Handle disputes.

• CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.

**9\. References**

**•** Ministry of Labour, Solidarity and Social Security: https://www.mtsss.gov.pt
