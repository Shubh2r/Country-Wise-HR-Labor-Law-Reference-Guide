**Country Name: Algeria**

**🇩🇿 Algeria – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Law No. 90‑11 (1990)_ – general framework for employment.
    *   _Executive Decree 26‑76 (2026)_ – new compliance requirements.
    *   _Social Security Code_.
    *   _Anti‑Narcotics Laws 04‑18, 23‑05, 25‑03_ (impacting workplace compliance).
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour, Employment and Social Security.
*   Applicability: Covers all employers — public, private, foreign branches, diplomatic missions, liaison offices.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent.
    *   Fixed‑term.
    *   Temporary/seasonal.
*   Probationary Periods: Typically 6 months; must be specified in contract.
*   Mandatory Clauses: job description, wages, working hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   New Requirement (2026): Negative drug‑screen certificate required for all candidates (local or foreign) before contract conclusion.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (5 days × 8 hours).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime on normal days.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → work on rest days/public holidays.
*   Rest Days: Friday is the statutory weekly rest day.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave: 30 calendar days/year (pro‑rated for partial service).
*   Public Holidays: 12–14 annually (national + religious).
*   Sick Leave: Paid, covered by social security after medical certification.
*   Maternity Leave: 14 weeks (paid).
*   Paternity Leave: 3 days (paid).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage (SNMG): Set by government; ~DZD 20,000/month (2025).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social Security (CNAS).
    *   Retirement fund.
    *   Work injury insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in public sector.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Period: 1 month (permanent contracts).
*   Severance Pay: 1 month’s wages per year of service (capped).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.
*   New Compliance (2026): Employers must update internal rules within 6 months of Decree 26‑76 or face penalties.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must provide resignation notice (same as employer notice periods).
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence, drug use) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate: Enforces compliance with Law 90‑11 and Decree 26‑76.
*   Penalties:
    *   Administrative fines for recruitment breaches.
    *   Criminal liability for negligence or unauthorised disclosure of drug‑test data.
*   Dispute Resolution: Labour courts handle disputes.

**9\. References**

*   Ministry of Labour, Employment and Social Security (Algeria).
*   Labour Law No. 90‑11 (1990).
*   Executive Decree 26‑76 (2026).
*   CNAS (Social Security Authority).

**🇩🇿 Additional Clauses & Sub‑Clauses You Should Know**

*   **Labour Law No. 90‑11 (1990)**:
    *   Governs employment contracts, working conditions, and dispute resolution.
*   **Executive Decree 26‑76 (2026)**:
    *   Requires employers to update internal rules within 6 months.
    *   Mandates negative drug‑screening certificates for all candidates (local and foreign).
    *   Introduces penalties for negligence or unauthorised disclosure of test data.
*   **Social Security Code**:
    *   Employer contributions ~26% of wages.
    *   Employee contributions ~9%.
*   **Foreign Employees**:
    *   Must obtain work permits.
    *   Subject to same drug‑screening and compliance requirements.
*   **Recent Developments**:
*   Minimum wage (SNMG) adjusted to ~DZD 20,000/month (2025).
*   Stronger compliance obligations under Decree 26‑76.
*   Expanded anti‑narcotics workplace compliance rules.
