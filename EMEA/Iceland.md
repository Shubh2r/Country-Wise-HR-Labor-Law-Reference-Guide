**Country Name: Iceland**

**🇮🇸 Iceland – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Act on Working Environment, Health and Safety_.
    *   _Act on Labour Market Rights_.
    *   _Holiday Act_.
    *   _Act on Equal Status and Equal Rights of Women and Men_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Directorate of Labour.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; collective agreements (CBAs) are central to wage and working condition regulation.
*   Recent Developments (2025–26):
*   Expanded parental leave entitlements.
*   Continued focus on gender equality and pay transparency.
*   Digitalisation of payroll and compliance systems.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment: Permanent, fixed‑term, temporary.
*   Probationary Periods: Typically 3 months (regulated by CBAs).
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Icelandic or English.
*   Written Contracts: Mandatory.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (often reduced to 37.5 via CBAs).
*   Overtime Rules: Paid at _1.038× to 1.25×_ hourly wage depending on CBA.
*   Rest Periods: 11 hours daily rest; 35 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave: 24 working days/year minimum (Holiday Act).
*   Public Holidays: ~12 annually (including Independence Day, Christmas, Easter).
*   Sick Leave: 2 days/month worked (paid, extended via CBAs).
*   Parental Leave: 12 months shared between parents (paid via social insurance).
*   Maternity Leave: 6 months (shared quota system).
*   Paternity Leave: 6 months (shared quota system).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: No statutory minimum; wages set by CBAs.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social Insurance: employer ~11.5%, employee ~4%.
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Holiday bonus and December bonus (mandatory under CBAs).

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   1 month (<5 years of service)
    *   3 months (≥10 years).
    *   Longer for senior employees under CBAs.
*   Severance Pay: Not statutory; negotiated in CBAs or contracts.
*   Termination Rules: Must be based on documented cause; consultation required.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Employees must contribute to social insurance.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Directorate of Labour: Oversees compliance.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.
