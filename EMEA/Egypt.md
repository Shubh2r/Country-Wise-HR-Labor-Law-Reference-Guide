**Country Name: Egypt**

**🇪🇬 Egypt – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Law No. 12 of 2003_ – core employment framework.
    *   _Social Insurance Law No. 148 of 2019_.
    *   _Civil Code_ (for general contractual obligations).
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Manpower.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees in private sector; public sector governed by separate civil service rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (must specify duration).
    *   Temporary/project‑based.
*   Probationary Periods: Up to 3 months; must be specified in contract.
*   Mandatory Clauses: job description, wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Arabic is mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed but Arabic version prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week maximum.
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.35×_ hourly wage → overtime on normal days.
    *   _1.7×_ hourly wage → overtime on rest days/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 1 day weekly rest (Friday is common).

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   21 working days/year (after 1 year service).
    *   30 days/year after 10 years service or age 50+.
*   Public Holidays: 12–14 annually (national + religious).
*   Sick Leave: Paid; 75% of wage for first 90 days, 85% thereafter (covered by social insurance).
*   Maternity Leave: 90 days (paid, up to 3 times during service).
*   Paternity Leave: Not statutory, but some employers provide short leave.
*   Parental Leave: Female employees may take up to 2 years unpaid leave for childcare (twice during service).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Set by government; ~EGP 3,500/month (2025).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social insurance (employer ~18.75%, employee ~11%).
    *   Health insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in some industries.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   2 months (<10 Years Service)
    *   3 months (≥10 years service).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   1 month’s wages per year of service (first 5 years).
    *   1.5 months’ wages per year thereafter.
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to social insurance.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate (Ministry of Manpower): Enforces compliance with labour law.
*   Penalties: Fines for breaches of working time, wage, and safety rules.
*   Dispute Resolution: Labour courts handle disputes.
