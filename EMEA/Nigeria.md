**Country Name: Nigeria**

**🇳🇬 Nigeria – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Act (2004)_ – core employment framework.
    *   _Trade Unions Act_.
    *   _Factories Act_ (health & safety).
    *   _Employee Compensation Act (2010)_.
    *   _Pension Reform Act (2014)_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Federal Ministry of Labour and Employment.
*   Applicability: Covers employees in formal sector; CBAs supplement statutory rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term.
    *   Casual (daily/seasonal).
    *   Apprenticeship.
*   Probationary Periods: Typically 3–6 months (not formally regulated).
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: English is mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (8 hours/day).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   No statutory overtime rate; determined by contract or collective agreement.
*   Rest Periods: 1 day weekly rest (Sunday common).

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   6 working days/year minimum (after 12 months service).
    *   Often extended to 15–20 days by contract/CBAs.
*   Public Holidays: 11 annually (e.g., Independence Day, Eid, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: 12 days/year minimum (with medical certificate).
*   Maternity Leave: 12 weeks (paid at 50% wages).
*   Paternity Leave: Recently introduced in some states (e.g., Lagos – 2 weeks).
*   Parental Leave: Not formally regulated; adoption leave varies by employer.

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted periodically (~₦30,000/month in 2025).
*   Overtime Pay: As per contract/CBAs.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Pension (employer 10%, employee 8%).
    *   National Housing Fund (NHF).
    *   PAYE income tax.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in practice.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods (Labour Act):
    *   1 day (<3 months service).
    *   1 week (3 months–2 years).
    *   2 weeks (2–5 years).
    *   1 month (≥5 years).
*   Severance Pay: Not statutory; redundancy compensation often negotiated via CBAs.
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to pension and NHF.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate (Ministry of Labour): Enforces compliance with labour law.
*   Industrial Arbitration Panel & National Industrial Court: Handle disputes.
*   Trade Unions & CBAs: Strong influence in wage and redundancy negotiations.

*   **Factories Act**: Workplace health and safety standards.
*   **Employee Compensation Act (2010)**: Compensation for workplace injuries.
*   **Pension Reform Act (2014)**: Mandatory contributory pension scheme.
*   **Language Requirement**: Contracts must be in English.
