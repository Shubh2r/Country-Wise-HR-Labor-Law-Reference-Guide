**Country Name: South Africa**

**🇿🇦 South Africa – HR & Labour Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Basic Conditions of Employment Act (BCEA)_ – core employment framework.
    *   _Labour Relations Act (LRA)_ – collective bargaining, unions, dispute resolution.
    *   _Employment Equity Act (EEA)_ – equality and non‑discrimination.
    *   _Occupational Health and Safety Act (OHSA)_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Department of Employment and Labour.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (allowed if justified; otherwise deemed permanent).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/seasonal work.
*   Probationary Periods: Reasonable duration (commonly 3–6 months).
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: English or local language; must be understandable to employee.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 45 hours/week (9 hours/day if working ≤5 days; 8 hours/day if working ≥6 days).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → overtime weekdays.
    *   _2×_ hourly wage → Sundays/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 12 hours daily rest; 36 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   21 consecutive days/year (≈15 working days).
*   Public Holidays: 12 annually (e.g., Freedom Day, Heritage Day, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: 30 days over a 3‑year cycle (paid).
*   Maternity Leave: 4 months (unpaid, but UIF benefits available).
*   Paternity/Parental Leave: 10 consecutive days (paid via UIF).
*   Family Responsibility Leave: 3 days/year (death of close family, child illness).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted annually (~ZAR 27.58/hour in 2025).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Unemployment Insurance Fund (UIF).
    *   Skills Development Levy (SDL).
    *   Compensation Fund (occupational injuries).
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common in practice.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods (BCEA):
    *   1 week (<6 months service)
    *   2 weeks (6–12 months).
    *   4 weeks (≥1 year).
*   Severance Pay:
    *   1 week’s wages per completed year of service (retrenchment/redundancy).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to UIF.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate (Department of Employment and Labour): Enforces compliance.
*   Commission for Conciliation, Mediation and Arbitration (CCMA): Handles disputes.
*   Labour Courts: Adjudicate complex cases.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.

*   **Employment Equity Act (EEA)**: Equality, affirmative action, reporting obligations.
*   **Occupational Health and Safety Act (OHSA)**: Workplace safety standards.
*   **Language Requirement**: Contracts must be understandable to the employee (English or local language).
