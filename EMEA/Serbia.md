**Country Name: Serbia**

**🇷🇸 Serbia – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

*   Primary Laws:
    *   _Labour Law (Zakon o radu)_ – core employment framework.
    *   _Law on Safety and Health at Work_.
    *   _Law on Pension and Disability Insurance_.
*   Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Labour, Employment, Veteran and Social Affairs.
*   Applicability: Covers all employees; CBAs supplement statutory rules.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

*   Types of Employment:
    *   Permanent (open‑ended).
    *   Fixed‑term (max 36 months).
    *   Part‑time.
    *   Temporary/seasonal work.
*   Probationary Periods: Max 6 months.
*   Mandatory Clauses: wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.
*   Language Requirement: Serbian is mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed but Serbian prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

*   Standard Hours: 40 hours/week (8 hours/day).
*   Overtime Rules:
    *   _1.26×_ hourly wage → overtime weekdays.
    *   _1.5×_ hourly wage → weekends/public holidays.
*   Rest Periods: 12 hours daily rest; 24 hours weekly rest.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

*   Annual Leave:
    *   20 working days/year minimum.
*   Public Holidays: 9 annually (e.g., New Year, Statehood Day, Easter, Christmas).
*   Sick Leave: Paid; employer covers first 30 days, then social insurance.
*   Maternity Leave: 1 year (paid at 100% of average wage).
*   Paternity Leave: 7 days (paid).
*   Parental Leave: Up to 365 days combined (job protected).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

*   Minimum Wage: Adjusted annually (~RSD 47,000/month gross in 2025).
*   Overtime Pay: As above.
*   Mandatory Contributions:
    *   Social security (employer ~17.9%, employee ~19.9%).
    *   Covers pension, health, unemployment, accident insurance.
*   Bonus/13th Month Pay: Not statutory, but common under CBAs.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

*   Notice Periods:
    *   15–30 days depending on tenure.
*   Severance Pay:
    *   1/3 month’s wages per year of service (redundancy).
*   Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

*   Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.
*   Must give contractual notice for resignation.
*   Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.
*   Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

*   Labour Inspectorate: Enforces compliance with labour law.
*   Labour Courts: Handle disputes.
*   CBAs: Binding and enforceable across industries.
