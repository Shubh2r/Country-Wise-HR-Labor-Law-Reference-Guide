**Country Name: Angola**

**🇦🇴 Angola – HR & Labor Law Guide (2026)**

**1\. Country Overview**

**•** Primary Laws:

• General Labour Law (Law No. 2/2000) – core employment framework.

• New Labour Law (2024 reform) – modernized rules on contracts, leave, and termination.

• Social Security Law.

• Regulatory Authority: Ministry of Public Administration, Labour and Social Security (MAPTSS).

• Applicability: Covers all employees, including expatriates, with strict compliance obligations.

**2\. Employment Contracts**

**•** Types of Employment:

Permanent.

Fixed‑term (up to 3 years, renewable).

Temporary/project‑based.

• Probationary Periods:

60 days for most employees.

Up to 6 months for managerial roles.

Mandatory Clauses: job description, wages, hours, leave, termination conditions.

Language Requirement: Portuguese is mandatory; bilingual contracts allowed but Portuguese version prevails.

**3\. Working Hours & Overtime**

**•** Standard Hours: 44 hours/week (8 hours/day).

• Overtime Rules:

1.25× hourly wage → overtime on normal days.

1.5× hourly wage → overtime on rest days.

2× hourly wage → overtime on public holidays.

• Rest Days: Sunday is the statutory weekly rest day.

**4\. Leave & Holidays**

**•** Annual Leave:

22 working days/year (after 12 months service).

Pro‑rated for partial service.

• Public Holidays: 12 annually (national + religious).

• Sick Leave: Paid, covered by social security after medical certification.

• Maternity Leave: 3 months (paid).

• Paternity Leave: 5 days (paid).

**5\. Compensation & Benefits**

**•** Minimum Wage: Sector‑based; adjusted periodically by government.

• Overtime Pay: As above.

• Mandatory Contributions:

• Social Security (employer ~8%, employee ~3%).

• Work injury insurance.

• Bonus/13th Month Pay: Common practice but not statutory.

**6\. Termination & Severance**

**•** Notice Period:

• 30 days for permanent contracts.

• 15 days for fixed‑term contracts.

• Severance Pay:

• 1 month’s wages per year of service (capped).

• Exemptions: No severance if dismissed for misconduct.

• Recent Reform (2024): Streamlined redundancy procedures and clarified severance entitlements.

**7\. Employee Obligations**

**•** Must comply with company rules and statutory obligations.

• Must provide resignation notice (same as employer notice periods).

• Misconduct (fraud, theft, violence) can justify dismissal without severance.

• Employees must contribute to social security.

**8\. Compliance & Enforcement**

**•** Labour Inspectorate (MAPTSS): Enforces compliance with labour law.

• Penalties: Steeper fines for late filings, unregistered employees, or non‑compliance.

• Dispute Resolution: Labour courts handle disputes.
